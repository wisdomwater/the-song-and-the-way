## 27 — Beyond the Veil

> *Desire narrows the eyes, but surrender teaches the heart to see.*

There are many ways a person begins searching for the Sacred. Some begin in sorrow after life breaks open in ways they did not expect. A loss arrives. A relationship fractures. The old certainties no longer hold together the way they once did. And suddenly the heart becomes willing to ask deeper questions it had avoided for years.

Others begin through longing.

A quiet ache follows them through ordinary life. Even beautiful moments feel incomplete somehow. Success does not satisfy the way they imagined it would. Pleasure fades too quickly. The soul hungers for something it cannot fully name, as though beneath every earthly desire there lives a deeper thirst waiting patiently to be discovered.

Others come through wonder. A sunrise glimpsed in silence before dawn. Music that opens something hidden inside the chest. The birth of a child. Wind moving through trees while the world suddenly feels alive with mystery. For a brief moment, the surface of life grows thin, and something vast seems to shimmer quietly beneath it.

And still others begin simply because they are tired.

Tired of carrying themselves alone. Tired of endless striving. Tired of the noise inside the mind and the exhausting pressure to constantly become more than they are. The ancient wisdom traditions honor all these beginnings, because every sincere longing can become a doorway. Even imperfect seeking contains grace.

Yet over time the spiritual life begins asking a deeper question: what is it we truly want?

At first many people seek the Sacred as a means toward something else. Relief from fear. Protection from suffering. Answers to uncertainty. A sense of control over life's unpredictability. This is natural. The heart reaches for help before it learns how to rest in love itself. But slowly the soul begins realizing that no temporary thing can fully carry the weight of its longing.

Achievement fades. Possessions lose their shine. Even joy cannot be held permanently in place. And so desire, when left unexamined, continually narrows vision. The mind becomes restless, always leaning toward what it does not yet possess, always imagining fulfillment somewhere just beyond the present moment. Life becomes filtered through endless calculations: What can I gain? What can I secure? What might protect me from loss?

This creates a kind of spiritual blindness. We stop seeing clearly because craving has taken hold of perception itself. But surrender begins loosening this veil. Not all at once. Slowly.

The heart learns to receive beautiful things without turning them into idols. To enjoy without clinging. To grieve without collapsing into despair. To love without possession or control. And as this happens, devotion itself begins changing. The soul no longer seeks only gifts from the Sacred. It begins seeking communion — presence, nearness, union with the deeper Love hidden beneath all appearances.

There is a profound difference between loving peace because it comforts us and loving truth because it is true. The first still revolves around the self. The second begins opening beyond it. And strangely, this surrender does not diminish life. It enlarges it. The heart becomes wider, quieter, less imprisoned by the endless urgency of desire.

The world remains beautiful, but it is no longer worshipped. Pleasure remains meaningful, but it no longer rules. Beneath all passing things the soul begins sensing something steadier — a Presence quietly holding everything together from within. Nearer than breath. More constant than emotion. Hidden beneath the changing surface of the world like a river flowing silently beneath ice.

The veil grows thin there.

And slowly, patiently, the heart begins learning how to see.

### Breath Prayer

> Inhale: Draw me beyond the veil
>
> Exhale: Let my heart seek You
