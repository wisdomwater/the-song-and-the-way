## 27 — Beyond the Veil

> *Desire narrows the eyes,  
> but surrender teaches the heart to see.*

## The Song

Krishna said:

The world is bewildered
by the woven powers of nature.

Goodness,
passion,
and darkness
move through all things.

They color perception.

They shape desire.

They cloud the heart.

And because of this,
people do not see Me clearly —
the One beyond these changing forces,
the One who does not pass away.

This veil is difficult to cross.

The patterns are deep.

The pull of the senses is strong.

The false self clings
to what it can name,
own,
use,
and control.

Yet those who take shelter in Me
can cross beyond the veil.

Some refuse this shelter.

Their wisdom has been covered.
Their hearts are turned away.
They become trapped
in the restless movements
of illusion.

But others come.

Some come because they suffer.

Pain has broken open
what comfort kept closed.

Some come because they wonder.

A question has begun burning within them.

Some come seeking provision.

They long for help,
stability,
and blessing.

And some come seeking truth itself.

They hunger to know
what is real beneath all passing things.

All who come are welcomed
as noble souls.

But Krishna said
the one who seeks truth in love
is especially dear.

The one whose mind remains turned toward Him.

The one whose devotion is steady.

The one who seeks not merely relief,
not merely answers,
not merely gifts,
but union.

Such a soul becomes deeply beloved.

And the Beloved becomes dearer
than all else.

After many births,
after many roads,
after many lessons learned through longing,
the wise finally surrender.

They come to see:

All things arise from the One.

All things rest in the One.

All things are held
within the mystery of the Beloved.

Such a soul is rare.

Yet many are carried away
by lesser desires.

They seek smaller powers
for smaller ends.

They place their faith
where their cravings lead them.

And even there,
Krishna said,
I sustain their faith.

Even their seeking unfolds
within My permission.

They receive what they pursue,
but the fruits are temporary.

What is gained through desire
eventually fades.

What is sought for the ego
cannot give the soul its rest.

The worshipper reaches
the object of their worship.

But the one who loves Me
comes to Me.

### Along the Way

This passage speaks tenderly
about the many ways people seek the Sacred.

Not everyone comes from the same place.

Some come because life has become unbearable.

Some come because questions will not leave them alone.

Some come because they need help.

Some come because truth itself
has become more beautiful than comfort.

The Gita does not mock these beginnings.

It honors them.

Distress can become a doorway.

Curiosity can become a lamp.

Need can become prayer.

Even imperfect seeking
may contain the first movements of grace.

But the passage also invites us
to notice what we are truly seeking.

Do we seek the Holy
only as a way to get what we want?

Do we pray only to secure outcomes?

Do we become devoted
only when devotion serves our desires?

This is not a reason for shame.

It is an invitation to honesty.

Much of the spiritual life
is the slow purification of desire.

At first,
we may seek peace
because we are tired.

We may seek wisdom
because life has confused us.

We may seek blessing
because we are afraid.

But over time,
love deepens.

The gift becomes less important
than the Giver.

Relief becomes less important
than communion.

Answers become less important
than trust.

The heart begins to want
not merely help from the Divine,
but life with the Divine.

This is where freedom begins.

Desire often narrows our vision.

It makes us grasp at partial goods
as if they were ultimate.

It makes temporary things carry
the weight of eternity.

And when those things fade,
we suffer.

But surrender widens the heart.

It teaches us to receive good things
without making them gods.

It teaches us to seek truth
beyond the immediate hunger
of the ego.

The veil is crossed
not by force of will alone,
but by refuge.

By turning again and again
toward the One
who is deeper than every mode,
larger than every desire,
and nearer than the breath.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Draw me beyond the veil”

Breathing out:
“Let my heart seek You”
