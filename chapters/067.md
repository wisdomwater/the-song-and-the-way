## 67 — The One Life Within All Beings

> *Wisdom begins when we stop seeing ourselves as separate from the rest of life.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke
of knowledge.

Not merely
information,
arguments,
or cleverness—

but the kind of seeing
that changes
how a person lives.

There is a knowledge,
He said,
that sees one undivided Life
moving through
all beings.

One eternal presence
appearing
through countless forms.

The same sacred breath
within the poor
and the powerful.

Within the stranger
and the beloved.

Within the animal,
the child,
the aging body,
the enemy,
and the self.

The forms differ.

The essence does not.

This vision,
Krishna said,
is wisdom born
of clarity.

But there is another way
of seeing.

A fragmented vision
that believes
every being
is fundamentally separate.

Separate interests.

Separate worth.

Separate importance.

This vision divides
life endlessly
into categories,
tribes,
hierarchies,
and identities.

And from this division
arise comparison,
fear,
competition,
and greed.

Then there is
an even darker form
of knowledge—

a narrow obsession
that clings blindly
to one tiny fragment
of reality
and mistakes it
for the whole truth.

A mind trapped
inside its own smallness.

Unable to see
beyond habit,
ego,
or inherited assumptions.

Krishna invites Arjuna
toward another way.

Toward the seeing
that recognizes
the One
moving through the many.

### Along the Way

This teaching feels
especially important now.

Modern life trains us
to fragment everything.

Political tribes.

Religious tribes.

Economic classes.

National identities.

Social algorithms
constantly sorting humanity
into “us”
and “them.”

And once division deepens,
compassion becomes harder.

It becomes easier
to dismiss suffering
when the suffering belongs
to “those people.”

But spiritual vision
moves in the opposite direction.

It softens the illusion
of separateness.

Not by erasing individuality—
Krishna is not saying
all people become identical—

but by recognizing
a deeper shared essence
beneath the surface differences.

This changes
how we treat others.

The cashier.

The exhausted coworker.

The homeless man.

The political opponent.

The aging parent.

The frightened child inside ourselves.

If the same sacred life
moves through all beings,
then cruelty toward others
eventually becomes
cruelty toward ourselves.

And compassion toward others
becomes a kind of participation
in divine reality itself.

This does not mean
we stop using discernment.

Boundaries still matter.

Justice still matters.

Wisdom still matters.

But the heart begins
to move differently.

Less from domination.

More from reverence.

Less from superiority.

More from kinship.

The soul slowly awakens
to the possibility
that beneath all the noise,
all the conflict,
all the labels—

there is one Life
breathing through everything.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“One Life moves through all”

Breathing out:
“Teach me to see with compassion”
