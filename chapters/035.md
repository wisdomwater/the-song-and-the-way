## 35 — The Lamp Within the Heart

> *The soul does not awaken through force alone.  
> Light is also given.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke
of those who remain near Him
through love.

Not occasionally.

Not only in moments of fear
or desperation.

But steadily.

With affection.

With sincerity.

With hearts that continue turning
toward the Eternal
again and again.

To such souls,
Krishna said,
I give the wisdom
that leads them closer to Me.

Not merely information.

Not cold knowledge.

But living insight.

A deeper seeing.

A guidance that quietly unfolds
within the heart itself.

And then Krishna spoke
with extraordinary tenderness.

Out of compassion,
I enter the darkness within them
and destroy ignorance
with the shining lamp
of understanding.

The image lingered.

Not condemnation.

Not rejection.

A lamp.

A light carried gently
into inner darkness.

The confusion of the mind.

The fears hidden deep within.

The endless wandering
of the restless self.

The Divine does not merely command
from a distance.

The Divine draws near.

Illuminating.

Healing.

Awakening.

And those who love the Eternal
begin to gather together.

They speak of what is sacred.

They remind one another.

They encourage one another
when the path feels difficult.

Their joy deepens
through shared remembrance.

The holy name is spoken.

Stories are carried.

Songs rise together.

And somewhere within all this,
the Presence draws near —
not through spectacle,
but through devotion.

Not through force,
but through love.

### Along the Way

This passage offers
a deeply hopeful vision
of spiritual growth.

Many people imagine transformation
as something they must accomplish
through sheer effort alone.

Try harder.

Control more.

Purify yourself completely.

Fix every flaw.

But the Gita presents
a more relational vision.

Yes,
practice matters.

Attention matters.

Discipline matters.

But awakening is not produced
through willpower alone.

There is also grace.

There is help.

There is illumination
that arrives as gift.

Anyone who has sincerely walked
a spiritual path for long enough
has likely experienced this mystery.

Moments of unexpected clarity.

A sudden softening of the heart.

Wisdom arriving quietly
after long confusion.

A deeper compassion
that seemed impossible before.

It can feel
as though light has entered
from somewhere beyond the grasp
of the ordinary ego.

The passage also reminds us
that spiritual life
is rarely meant to be lived alone.

People awaken together.

They strengthen one another.

They speak truth
when another forgets.

They sing,
pray,
listen,
weep,
and hope together.

Sacred conversation itself
can become transformative.

Not argument.

Not performance.

But honest shared remembrance.

And perhaps this is why
the image of the lamp matters so deeply.

Darkness is not removed
through hatred of darkness.

It is removed
through light.

The healing of the soul
often happens the same way.

Not through endless self-condemnation.

But through gradually allowing
truth,
love,
mercy,
and awareness
to illuminate
what was once hidden.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Light the lamp within me”

Breathing out:
“Lead me out of darkness”
