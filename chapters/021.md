## 21 - The Light Behind the Eyes

> *There is a quiet joy beneath the noise of craving. A deeper life waits beneath the surface self.*

Most people spend much of their lives pulled outward.

Toward achievement.
Toward distraction.
Toward pleasure.
Toward the endless movement of thought and emotion.

The mind becomes crowded.

One moment longing.
Another moment fearing.
Another comparing,
grasping,
regretting,
anticipating.

And beneath all this movement, the soul quietly grows tired.

Not because life itself is wrong,
but because the self was never meant to live entirely upon the surface of things.

There is a deeper place within us.

Still.
Spacious.
Unhurried.

A place that remains present even while thoughts rise and fall like weather across the sky.

The ancient teachers often spoke of awakening as remembering this deeper center.

Not creating it.
Not achieving it.

Remembering it.

For most suffering begins when we mistake every passing impulse, emotion, or external circumstance for the whole of who we are.

A painful thought appears:
“I am a failure.”

Fear rises:
“I am unsafe.”

Desire surges:
“I must have this to be whole.”

And slowly the self becomes entangled in these movements, forgetting the quiet awareness beneath them.

But something changes when inner seeing begins to awaken.

The soul no longer clings so desperately to every passing experience.

Pleasure is received gratefully,
without demanding it last forever.

Pain is felt honestly,
without believing it defines the whole of existence.

Praise no longer intoxicates the heart so easily.
Criticism no longer destroys it completely.

A deeper steadiness begins forming.

Like roots growing silently beneath the earth.

This inward awakening also changes the way we see others.

The ego constantly measures:
Who matters more?
Who matters less?
Who is successful?
Who is useful?
Who deserves attention?

But wisdom softens this instinct to rank and divide.

For when a person begins touching the deeper life within themselves, they start recognizing it hidden within others too.

Behind every face lives unseen sorrow.
Unspoken longing.
Fragile hope.
The quiet desire to be loved and safe.

The outer forms differ greatly.

Yet something sacred moves through all beings alike.

And this realization changes the texture of compassion.

Others are no longer abstractions,
competitors,
or obstacles.

They become fellow travelers carrying invisible burdens.

The spiritual life also changes our relationship with pleasure itself.

Not by rejecting beauty,
joy,
or delight.

But by no longer asking temporary experiences to carry the weight of ultimate fulfillment.

The warmth of sunlight.
Music drifting through a room.
Laughter shared around a table.
The scent of rain upon dry ground.

These things become gifts instead of possessions.

Moments to receive gently rather than cling to desperately.

And in this loosening,
another kind of joy quietly appears.

Not dramatic.
Not performative.

A quieter radiance.

The simple peace of no longer needing life to constantly prove your worth.

The quiet relief of resting, even briefly, beneath the noise of the surface self.

Like light slowly emerging through morning clouds.

Already present all along.

### Breath Prayer

> Inhale: *There is light within me*
>
> Exhale: *I rest beneath the noise*
