## 21 — The Light Behind the Eyes

> *Beneath the restless surface of the self, a quieter presence has been waiting all along.*

Most people spend much of their lives being pulled outward. Toward achievement, distraction, pleasure, anxiety, comparison, endless movement. The mind becomes crowded with voices demanding attention. One moment grasping for what it does not have, another moment fearing what may come next. Beneath all this noise, something within us quietly grows weary.

Not because life itself is wrong, but because we were never meant to live entirely upon the surface of things.

There is a deeper place within us. Still. Spacious. Unhurried. A place that remains present even while thoughts and emotions move like weather across the sky. The ancient teachers often spoke of awakening not as creating this deeper center, but remembering it. Returning to something that has always been there beneath the noise.

Much of human suffering begins when we mistake every passing thought or emotion for the whole of who we are. A fearful thought arises, and we believe fear has become our identity. Shame appears, and we assume we are permanently broken. Desire surges through the body, and we begin arranging our lives around the promise that fulfillment must lie just beyond the next possession, achievement, or experience.

And slowly, without noticing it, the self becomes tangled in constant reaction.

But when inner seeing begins to awaken, something starts to loosen. Thoughts still arise. Emotions still move through the body. Pleasure still comes. Pain still arrives uninvited. Yet the soul no longer clings quite so desperately to each passing wave. Joy is received gratefully without demanding it remain forever. Sorrow is felt honestly without believing it defines the entirety of existence.

A deeper steadiness begins forming quietly beneath ordinary life. Like roots spreading beneath the earth where no one can see them. Praise no longer intoxicates the heart so completely. Criticism no longer destroys it so easily. There is more room now. More breath. More silence between impulse and response.

This awakening also changes the way we begin seeing others. The ego is always measuring and dividing. Who matters more? Who matters less? Who is successful? Who is worthy of attention? But wisdom softens this habit of separation. For when we begin touching the deeper life within ourselves, we slowly recognize that same hidden depth within others too.

Behind every face lives unseen sorrow. Unspoken longing. Fragile hope. The quiet desire to feel safe, loved, and at peace. The outer forms of human life may differ greatly, yet something sacred moves quietly through all beings alike. And when this begins to be seen, compassion becomes less performative and more natural. Others are no longer obstacles or abstractions. They become fellow travelers carrying invisible burdens through a difficult and beautiful world.

The spiritual life also changes our relationship with pleasure itself. Not by rejecting beauty or delight, but by no longer demanding that temporary things carry the weight of ultimate fulfillment. The warmth of sunlight through a window. Laughter shared across a table. Music drifting softly through a room. Rain falling upon dry ground after a long season of heat. These moments become gifts to receive gently instead of possessions to cling to desperately.

And in this loosening, another kind of joy quietly appears. Not dramatic. Not loud. A softer radiance beneath the surface self. The relief of no longer needing life to constantly prove your worth. The peace of resting, even briefly, beneath the endless movement of thought and craving.

Like light slowly emerging through morning clouds.

Already present all along.

### Breath Prayer

> Inhale: There is light within me
>
> Exhale: I rest beneath the noise
