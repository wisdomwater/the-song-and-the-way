## 45 — The Presence Within All Things

> *The Holy cannot be contained, yet nothing exists outside it.  
> The Eternal is nearer than breath and wider than the stars.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke
of the Presence
hidden within all life.

Its hands are everywhere.

Its feet move
through every road,
every creature,
every unfolding moment.

Its eyes
look out
through countless forms.

Its ears
hear every cry,
every prayer,
every whispered grief.

Nothing exists
outside its reach.

And yet,
this Presence
cannot be captured
by the senses.

It has no material form,
yet all forms arise within it.

It touches everything
while remaining untouched.

It sustains the world
without clinging to it.

It shines
through every quality of life—
beauty,
strength,
wisdom,
love—
yet remains beyond them all.

Krishna said:

It is both near
and impossibly far.

Closer
than your own breath.

Yet vast beyond imagining.

It dwells
within every moving being
and every silent stone.

Within birth.

Within death.

Within motion.

Within stillness.

Though it appears divided
among countless creatures,
it remains undivided.

One Presence
shining through many lives.

The source.

The sustainer.

The receiver of all things
when they pass away.

Like the ocean
holding every wave
without becoming separate from them.

Like sunlight
falling across many windows
while remaining one light.

And Krishna spoke
as if trying to guide the heart
toward something
that cannot fully be explained—
only encountered.

A Presence
hidden inside all things,
waiting quietly
to be seen.

### Along the Way

The ego sees separation everywhere.

My life.

My pain.

My success.

My people.

My enemies.

The mind divides the world endlessly,
building walls
between self and other.

But this passage invites us
into another way of seeing.

A deeper unity
beneath the surface of things.

Not sameness.

Not the erasing of individuality.

But the recognition
that all life
is held within
a greater Presence.

This changes
how we move through the world.

If the sacred dwells
within all beings,
then cruelty becomes harder to justify.

Contempt begins to soften.

Possession loosens.

Humility grows.

We begin to understand
that no person
is merely an object
for our anger,
use,
or dismissal.

Each carries
something immeasurably deep within them—
even when hidden beneath fear,
confusion,
or brokenness.

This passage also speaks
to the longing
many people quietly carry:

the longing to feel
that they are not abandoned
inside an indifferent universe.

Krishna describes a Presence
that is both transcendent
and intimate.

Beyond comprehension,
yet astonishingly close.

Near in silence.

Near in suffering.

Near in love.

Near in the breath.

Most of us search constantly
for certainty,
control,
and visible proof.

But the deepest realities
are often subtle.

Quiet.

Difficult to grasp
with the analytical mind alone.

Like love.

Like beauty.

Like peace.

They cannot always be dissected,
yet they can be known.

Not through possession,
but through participation.

Through stillness.

Through attention.

Through awakening.

And perhaps this is why
the soul grows calmer
when it finally stops trying
to control the sacred
and instead learns
to rest within it.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“You are near”

Breathing out:
“In all things”
