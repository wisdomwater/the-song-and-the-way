## 55 — The Hidden Light Within All Things

> *The sacred is not far away.  
> It breathes through memory, sunlight, hunger, and the quiet pulse of life itself.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke
of the soul
moving from life to life.

The deeper self
passes through bodies
as wind carries fragrance
from one place to another.

The senses follow.

The mind follows.

Desires,
attachments,
and impressions
travel onward
through the long journey
of becoming.

Yet most people
do not notice this mystery.

They see only surfaces.

Bodies.

Appearances.

Movements.

But the wise learn
to look more deeply.

The sincere seeker,
quiet in spirit,
begins to perceive
the hidden life within life.

The invisible presence
beneath the visible form.

Then Krishna spoke
of Himself
shining through creation.

I am the light
within the sun.

The radiance
within the moon.

The warmth
within the fire.

I enter the earth
and sustain all living things.

I nourish the plants.

I move within the rhythms
of growth and harvest.

I dwell within the body
as the fire of digestion,
transforming food into life.

And deeper still,
Krishna said:

I dwell
within every heart.

From Me arise memory,
understanding,
and even forgetfulness.

All wisdom,
all searching,
all sacred longing—
ultimately moves toward Me.

The divine was not described
as distant only,
but intimately woven
through every aspect
of existence.

The light in the sky.

The breath in the lungs.

The hunger in the body.

The knowing in the mind.

The quiet awareness
within the heart.

### Along the Way

Many people imagine
the sacred
as something distant,
hidden far away
from ordinary life.

But this passage moves
in the opposite direction.

Krishna points
toward a Presence
woven through the fabric
of existence itself.

The sunlight that warms the earth.

The rhythms of nature.

The body’s hidden intelligence.

The mysterious spark
of awareness and memory.

The sacred is not presented
as separate from life,
but as quietly sustaining it.

This changes
how we encounter the world.

Ordinary moments
become less ordinary.

Breathing.

Eating.

Walking beneath the moon.

Watching light move
through trees.

Listening deeply
to another human being.

All of life begins
to shimmer
with hidden depth.

This passage also invites humility.

Modern life often encourages us
to imagine ourselves
as entirely self-made.

But Krishna reminds us
how much of life
is received rather than controlled.

We do not create
the beating heart.

We do not command
the sunrise.

We do not fully understand
the mystery
of consciousness itself.

Even memory
can appear and disappear
without our consent.

There is something sacred
beneath our striving.

Something carrying life
moment by moment.

Krishna’s words about remembrance
are especially tender.

Anyone who has struggled,
suffered,
or wandered spiritually
knows how easily
the deeper truths of life
can be forgotten.

We become distracted.

Pulled outward.

Consumed by fear,
ambition,
resentment,
or exhaustion.

And yet the sacred Presence
does not abandon the soul.

It remains quietly near,
continuing to call us
toward awakening.

Toward remembrance.

Toward the deeper light
already hidden
within all things.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“You are near in all things”

Breathing out:
“Awaken my heart to see”
