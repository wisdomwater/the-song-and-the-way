## 34 — The Heart Turned Toward the Eternal

> *No one is outside the reach of grace.  
> The way remains open to every soul.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke plainly now.

Think of Me.

Love Me.

Offer your life toward Me.

Bow your heart
toward the Eternal,
and you will find your way home.

The invitation was simple.

Not reserved
for the powerful,
the educated,
the spiritually accomplished,
or the socially honored.

Open to all.

The Divine does not belong
to a single tribe,
nation,
class,
or caste.

No soul stands outside
the reach of sacred love.

And Krishna spoke again
of His vastness.

The sages do not fully know My origin,
He said.

Even the shining beings
cannot trace My beginning.

For I am before beginnings.

Birthless.

Endless.

The source
from which all things arise.

From Him come
wisdom
and confusion,
courage
and fear,
joy
and sorrow,
strength
and tenderness.

All the changing movements
of human life
rise like waves
from a deeper ocean.

And still,
the Eternal remains.

Those who awaken to this,
Krishna said,
begin to live differently.

They speak often
of what is sacred.

They encourage one another.

Their hearts grow joyful
through remembrance.

Their lives become rooted
in devotion
rather than fear.

And Krishna said:

I am the source of all things.

Everything flows from Me.

The wise who understand this
love Me
with all their hearts.

### Along the Way

This passage carries
a deep sense of welcome.

Human beings create endless systems
for deciding who belongs.

Who is worthy.

Who is pure enough.

Who deserves nearness to the sacred.

But the Gita continually breaks open
these narrow boundaries.

The invitation toward the Divine
is not based upon status,
achievement,
or social position.

It is based upon the turning
of the heart.

This matters deeply.

Many people quietly believe
they are disqualified from spiritual life.

Because of shame.

Because of failure.

Because of past wounds.

Because they do not fit
someone else's image
of holiness.

But the Gita speaks against despair.

No sincere movement toward truth,
love,
or surrender
is wasted.

The door remains open.

Again and again.

The passage also reminds us
that the Divine cannot be reduced
to something fully manageable
by the human mind.

We often want certainty
that can be controlled.

Definitions that remove mystery.

Systems that make reality feel tidy.

But the sacred always exceeds
our categories.

The Infinite cannot be contained
inside intellectual mastery alone.

This does not mean
understanding is unimportant.

But it does mean
that humility matters.

Wonder matters.

Reverence matters.

There is a kind of wisdom
that grows only
when the heart softens enough
to stand before mystery
without needing to conquer it.

And perhaps this is why
devotion keeps returning
throughout the Gita.

Not as emotional excess.

Not as blind obedience.

But as relational openness.

A life gradually reoriented
toward the Eternal.

A steady remembering.

A growing trust.

A heart learning,
little by little,
to rest in something larger
than itself.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Turn my heart toward You”

Breathing out:
“Teach me to rest in love”
