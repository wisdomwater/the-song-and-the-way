## 52 — The Unshaken Heart

> *Freedom is not the absence of difficulty.  
> It is the quiet stability that remains within it.*

## The Song

Arjuna asked:

How does one recognize
the person
who has gone beyond
the restless currents
of the world?

What does such a soul
look like?

How do they live?

Krishna answered
with the portrait
of a deeply rooted heart.

The liberated soul
does not cling
even to illumination.

Nor does it collapse
when confusion appears.

It does not desperately chase
clarity,
pleasure,
or emotional certainty.

And it does not panic
when darkness passes through.

It watches
without becoming possessed.

Like someone sitting quietly
on the riverbank,
watching the waters move.

The currents rise.

The currents fall.

But the witness remains steady.

Krishna said:

Such a person
is not shaken
by praise or blame.

Not inflated by honor.

Not crushed by insult.

Pleasure comes.

Pain comes.

Gain comes.

Loss comes.

Yet inwardly,
the soul remains rooted.

Not cold.

Not indifferent.

But free from being ruled
by every passing wave.

Gold and stone
lose their power
to define worth.

Approval and rejection
lose their power
to define identity.

Friend and enemy
are no longer approached
through the fever
of ego and resentment.

The heart becomes spacious.

Steady.

Quietly awake.

This person,
Krishna said,
has crossed beyond
the forces
that bind the soul.

### Along the Way

This teaching can sound impossible
at first.

Most of us are deeply shaped
by praise and criticism.

A kind word lifts us.

A harsh word wounds us.

We feel secure
when life moves in our favor,
and fearful
when it does not.

The ego constantly searches
for stability
through external conditions.

Recognition.

Possessions.

Success.

Control.

But Krishna describes
another kind of stability.

An inner rootedness
that does not depend entirely
on circumstances.

This does not mean
becoming emotionally numb.

It does not mean
pretending pain does not hurt.

Rather,
it means learning
not to surrender
the whole soul
to every passing emotion,
opinion,
or outcome.

The spiritually mature person
still feels sorrow.

Still feels joy.

Still experiences life deeply.

But there is space
around those experiences.

Awareness remains present.

The heart no longer reacts
with total compulsion.

This freedom changes
how we move through conflict.

When identity is rooted only
in ego,
we become highly defensive.

Every disagreement
feels threatening.

Every criticism
feels devastating.

Every insult
demands retaliation.

But when the soul becomes steadier,
it gains the ability
to respond
rather than merely react.

Krishna’s image
of seeing gold and stone alike
points toward freedom
from compulsive valuation.

The world constantly teaches us
to measure worth externally.

More status.

More wealth.

More admiration.

But none of these things
can anchor the soul permanently.

The unshaken heart
is not careless toward life.

It simply no longer worships
what constantly changes.

And perhaps this is
what real freedom begins to feel like:

Not domination.

Not emotional suppression.

But deep inward steadiness.

A quiet center
that remains present
through both storm and sunlight.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Root me in what is lasting”

Breathing out:
“Steady my heart in peace”
