## 7 - What Cannot Be Destroyed

> *Peace begins when we stop building our identity upon what was never meant to last.*

Human life changes constantly.

Bodies age.
Relationships shift.
Dreams appear and disappear.
Entire seasons of life rise and fall without asking permission from the heart.

One day we feel strong and certain.
Another day grief arrives unexpectedly and rearranges the inner landscape completely.

Most suffering comes not only from pain itself, but from our desperate attempt to hold still what was always moving.

We cling to youth as though it could remain forever.
We build identities around success, usefulness, beauty, certainty, or belonging.
We try to secure permanence within a world made of passing forms.

And because everything changes, fear quietly follows us everywhere.

The mind begins bargaining with time.

“If I can just keep this.”
“If I can avoid loss.”
“If I can protect what I love from change.”

Yet life continues moving like a river no hand can fully grasp.

There is a deeper wisdom that begins to emerge when the soul grows weary of trying to anchor itself in temporary things.

Beneath all change, something remains.

Not the personality constantly adapting to circumstance.
Not the emotions that rise and fall like weather.
Not the image we present to the world.

Something quieter.

A presence beneath experience itself.

Most people catch glimpses of it only briefly. In moments of stillness. In deep grief. In silence after prayer. While watching sunlight move across a wall or standing beneath a sky vast enough to dissolve the illusion of control.

For a moment, the restless self softens.

And something deeper simply witnesses.

This deeper awareness remains through every season of life.

The child changes.
The body changes.
Beliefs evolve.
The heart breaks and heals and breaks again.

Yet something continues quietly beneath it all, observing every transformation without being destroyed by any of them.

To remember this changes the way we suffer.

Pain still hurts.
Loss still matters.
Love still carries vulnerability.

But the soul no longer believes every passing wave is the whole ocean.

Pleasure becomes something to receive gratefully rather than cling to desperately.
Sorrow becomes something to move through rather than an identity to inhabit forever.

This is not emotional detachment.

It is steadiness.

The mature heart learns how to remain present within changing circumstances without collapsing every time life shifts shape.

Modern culture trains people to fear discomfort constantly. We distract ourselves endlessly because silence threatens to reveal how fragile our constructed identities truly are. We chase comfort, approval, certainty, and control hoping they will protect us from mortality and change.

But nothing temporary can fully hold the weight of the eternal longing within the human soul.

Eventually life teaches this to everyone.

The invitation is not to reject the world or stop loving deeply. It is to love without trying to possess. To participate fully in life while remembering that no outward form can become the final foundation of peace.

What changes will always change.

What passes will always pass.

But there is something within you that is older than fear and deeper than loss.

Something untouched by the constant movement of the world.

And when the heart begins resting there, even sorrow starts to loosen its grip.

### Breath Prayer

> Inhale: *I am more than this moment*
>
> Exhale: *Help me rest in what endures*
