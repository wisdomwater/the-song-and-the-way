## 7 — What Cannot Be Destroyed

> *Peace begins when we stop building our identity upon what was never meant to last.*

Human life is always changing. Bodies age. Relationships shift. Dreams appear and disappear like seasons passing through the landscape of the soul. One day we feel strong and certain. Another day grief arrives unexpectedly and rearranges the inner world completely. Nothing remains fixed for long, no matter how tightly we try to hold it.

Much of human suffering comes not only from pain itself, but from our desperate attempt to keep still what was always moving. We cling to youth as though it could remain forever. We build identities around success, usefulness, beauty, certainty, or belonging. We search for permanence inside things that were never designed to carry such weight. And because everything changes, fear quietly follows us everywhere.

The mind begins bargaining with time. *If I can just keep this. If I can avoid loss. If I can protect what I love from change.* Yet life continues moving like a river no hand can fully grasp. No amount of effort can prevent the passing of seasons. No amount of control can make temporary things eternal.

Eventually the soul grows weary of trying.

And in that weariness, another kind of wisdom slowly begins to emerge.

Beneath all change, something remains.

Not the personality constantly adapting to circumstance. Not the emotions that rise and fall like weather. Not the image presented to the world. Something quieter. A presence beneath experience itself. Most people glimpse it only briefly—in deep stillness, in grief, in silence after prayer, while watching light move slowly across a wall, or standing beneath a sky vast enough to dissolve the illusion of control.

For a moment, the restless self softens.

And something deeper simply witnesses.

This deeper awareness remains through every season of life. The child changes. The body changes. Beliefs evolve. The heart breaks and heals and breaks again. Yet something continues quietly beneath it all, observing every transformation without being destroyed by any of them. It is present in joy and sorrow alike. Present before success arrives and after success fades. Present even when identity itself begins to unravel.

To remember this changes the way we suffer. Pain still hurts. Loss still matters. Love still carries vulnerability. But the soul no longer mistakes every passing wave for the whole ocean. Pleasure becomes something to receive gratefully rather than cling to desperately. Sorrow becomes something to move through rather than a permanent identity.

This is not emotional detachment.

It is steadiness.

The mature heart learns how to remain present within changing circumstances without collapsing each time life shifts shape. It learns how to love deeply without trying to possess what cannot ultimately be kept. It learns how to stand inside impermanence without losing contact with what endures beneath it.

Modern life trains people to fear discomfort constantly. We distract ourselves endlessly because silence threatens to reveal how fragile our constructed identities truly are. We chase approval, certainty, comfort, and control hoping they will protect us from mortality and change. Yet nothing temporary can fully hold the eternal longing carried within the human spirit.

Eventually life teaches this to everyone.

The invitation is not to reject the world or stop loving deeply. It is to participate fully in life while remembering that no outward form can become the final foundation of peace. What changes will always change. What passes will always pass. But there is something within you that is older than fear and deeper than loss.

And when the heart begins resting there, even sorrow slowly loosens its grip.

### Breath Prayer

> Inhale: I am more than this moment
>
> Exhale: Help me rest in what endures
