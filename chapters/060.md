## 60 — The Shape of Faith

> *What we trust ourselves to shapes who we become.  
> Faith is not only what we believe, but how we live.*

## The Song

Arjuna asked Krishna:

What of those
who worship sincerely,
yet without wisdom?

What becomes
of devotion
when it is mixed
with confusion,
desire,
or ignorance?

Krishna answered:

Faith takes many forms.

And each person’s faith
grows from the condition
of their consciousness.

For a human being
slowly becomes
what they give themselves to.

Some are drawn
toward what is luminous.

Toward truth,
clarity,
compassion,
and reverence.

Others are driven
by ambition,
power,
or restless craving.

Still others drift
into darkness,
fear,
superstition,
or destructive obsession.

The outer forms may appear religious,
yet the inner movement
of the heart differs greatly.

Krishna warned
against harsh forms
of spirituality
born from pride and ego.

Those who torture themselves
in pursuit of power,
status,
or superiority
lose touch
with the sacred presence
already living within them.

The body itself,
He said,
is not meant to be despised.

Nor is spiritual practice
meant to become violence
against oneself.

The path of awakening
does not grow
through self-hatred.

True devotion
softens the heart.

Clarifies the mind.

Purifies intention.

It does not inflate the ego.

It does not make a person
harder,
crueler,
or more self-righteous.

The soul becomes shaped
by what it trusts,
what it worships,
and what it repeatedly turns toward.

And so Krishna invited Arjuna
to cultivate
a faith rooted in clarity,
humility,
and truth.

### Along the Way

This passage offers
a subtle understanding
of faith.

Faith is not merely
a set of doctrines
or religious labels.

It is the deep orientation
of the heart.

Everyone lives by faith
in something.

Some place their trust
in wealth.

Some in power.

Some in intellect.

Some in pleasure.

Some in ideology.

Some in compassion,
truth,
and the sacred.

Over time,
what we trust most
begins to shape
our character.

Krishna’s insight
is deeply psychological.

A fearful heart
develops one kind of faith.

An ambitious heart
develops another.

A peaceful
and compassionate heart
develops yet another.

This is why spiritual practice
cannot be separated
from inner transformation.

It is possible
to appear religious outwardly
while remaining inwardly
driven by ego,
control,
or insecurity.

The ego can even use spirituality
to feel superior.

To perform holiness.

To seek admiration.

To dominate others.

Krishna warns especially
against forms of devotion
that become harsh,
punishing,
or disconnected
from love.

Any spirituality
that increases cruelty,
self-hatred,
arrogance,
or contempt
has lost contact
with wisdom.

True spiritual depth
makes a person
more human,
not less.

More compassionate.

More honest.

More grounded.

More capable
of gentleness and humility.

The question beneath this passage
is not merely:

“What do I claim to believe?”

But rather:

“What is my life
actually being formed into?”

Because the soul
slowly takes the shape
of whatever it repeatedly worships.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Form my heart in truth”

Breathing out:
“Lead me in gentle wisdom”
