## 25 - No Sincere Step Is Lost

> *The soul that turns toward the Light is never abandoned on the way.*

Many people carry a quiet fear beneath their spiritual longing.

What if I never truly change?

What if my heart desires peace,
but my mind keeps wandering?

What if I begin sincerely,
only to lose courage,
lose focus,
lose clarity,
again and again?

The soul becomes discouraged by its own inconsistency.

One season feels filled with devotion and openness.
Another feels scattered and dry.
Moments of insight fade.
Old habits return.
The inner life can seem painfully unfinished.

And because people often imagine spiritual growth as steady ascent,
they begin secretly believing that every stumble means failure.

But the deeper wisdom traditions speak with far more mercy than this.

They remind us that transformation rarely unfolds in a straight line.

The soul learns slowly.

Like roots growing underground long before green shoots appear above the earth.

Nothing sincere is wasted.

No honest prayer disappears into emptiness.
No act of compassion is forgotten.
No longing for truth vanishes simply because confusion returns.

Even small movements toward love leave traces within us.

A quiet moment of surrender.
A breath taken instead of a harsh word.
A return to stillness after months of distraction.
The decision to begin again after shame says not to bother.

These things matter more than we realize.

For the heart remembers what it has truly touched.

Many people know the experience of being drawn back toward something deeper after long seasons away.

A sudden hunger for silence.
A longing to pray again.
A tenderness that reappears unexpectedly after years of numbness.

It can feel almost like remembering something ancient.

As though the soul carries within it a hidden memory of home.

And perhaps it does.

The sacred has a way of calling us gently back to ourselves.

Again and again.

Not with condemnation,
but with patience.

This does not mean the path requires no effort.

Practice still matters.

Attention still matters.

The inner life deepens through returning repeatedly to what is real.

Yet there is profound freedom in realizing that spiritual maturity is not built entirely upon perfection.

It is built through sincerity.

Through willingness.
Through humility.
Through staying open enough to begin again.

The ego often imagines growth as becoming impressive.

But the deeper life often looks much quieter than that.

More honest.
More surrendered.
More willing to stand without pretending.

Sometimes the holiest moment in a person’s life is simply this:
refusing to give up on love after disappointment.
Refusing to give up on truth after confusion.
Refusing to give up on tenderness after pain.

The soul grows through these hidden fidelities.

And slowly, over time,
knowledge becomes something warmer than certainty.

It becomes trust.

A resting of the heart inside something larger than fear and striving.

Not a cold belief held tightly in the mind,
but a relationship.

A sense of being carried even while unfinished.

Perhaps this is one of the deepest forms of grace:
that we are not required to become flawless before we are welcomed.

We are simply asked to keep turning toward the Light.

### Breath Prayer

> Inhale: *No sincere step is lost*
>
> Exhale: *I am held on the way*
