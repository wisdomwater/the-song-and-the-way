## 25 — No Sincere Step Is Lost

> *The soul that turns toward the Light  
> is never abandoned on the way.*

## The Song

Arjuna listened
to the path Krishna had described —
the steady mind,
the disciplined heart,
the soul gathered into union.

And a fear rose in him.

What happens,
he asked,
to the one who begins with faith
but cannot finish?

What becomes of the person
whose heart turns toward the sacred,
yet whose mind wanders?

Who reaches for stillness,
but falls back into distraction?

Who longs for freedom,
but does not attain perfection?

Does such a one become lost?

Like a broken cloud
scattered across the sky —
belonging neither to earth
nor to heaven?

Arjuna brought his doubt honestly.

He did not hide it.

He did not pretend
that the path was easy.

He placed his fear before Krishna
and asked for it to be removed.

And Krishna answered with tenderness:

No one who seeks the good
is ever destroyed.

Not in this life.

Not beyond it.

No sincere movement toward truth
is wasted.

No act of goodness disappears.

No longing for the Eternal
falls into nothing.

The one who falls from the path
is not abandoned.

The soul is carried onward
by what it has truly loved.

What was begun in one season
is remembered in another.

What was practiced imperfectly
still leaves its fragrance.

What the heart once reached for
quietly calls again.

Such a soul,
Krishna said,
finds its way back
to conditions where the journey can continue.

Born again into places of blessing,
or among those who already know the path,
the seeker feels an old pull awaken.

A memory deeper than thought.

A hunger that did not begin yesterday.

Even without fully knowing why,
they are drawn again
toward wisdom,
practice,
and union.

The work resumes.

The soul continues.

Through many beginnings,
many returns,
many purifications,
the seeker moves toward the Supreme.

So Krishna said to Arjuna:

Become a yogi.

Greater than harsh austerity.

Greater than mere intellectual pursuit.

Greater than ritual action alone
is the one who seeks union.

And among all yogis,
the deepest is the one
whose inner being rests in Me,
who turns with faith,
who loves with the whole heart.

Then Krishna began to open
a new depth of the teaching.

Listen, He said.

Let your mind be attached to Me.

Take refuge in Me.

Practice in this shelter,
and you will come to know Me —
not as an idea only,
but as reality.

Knowledge will become realization.

The truth will no longer remain
outside the heart.

### Along the Way

This passage is full of mercy
for anyone who has ever felt
they were failing spiritually.

Arjuna asks the question
many sincere people carry quietly:

What if I do not become
what I hoped to become?

What if my faith is real,
but my mind is scattered?

What if I begin again and again,
and still feel unfinished?

The answer Krishna gives
is profoundly gentle.

Nothing sincere is wasted.

The spiritual life is not measured
only by visible success.

It is also shaped by longing,
return,
practice,
humility,
and the hidden movements of the heart.

We often judge ourselves
by completion.

By consistency.

By whether we have arrived.

But the deeper life
may be working in us
even through imperfect beginnings.

A prayer half-spoken.

A desire to be free.

A moment of compassion
when resentment would have been easier.

A return after wandering.

These things matter.

They leave traces in the soul.

The passage also helps us see
that failure is not always final.

Sometimes what looks like falling away
is part of a longer unfolding.

The heart may lose focus.

The mind may grow tired.

Old desires may return.

But grace has a memory deeper than shame.

What is true in us
continues calling.

This does not make practice unnecessary.

Krishna still says:
Become a yogi.

Keep walking.

Keep returning.

Keep giving your life
to what is most real.

But he removes despair from the path.

The seeker is not a broken cloud
vanishing into emptiness.

The seeker is held.

Carried.

Invited again.

And at the end of the chapter,
the teaching becomes beautifully simple.

The highest path is not cold discipline alone.

Not achievement.

Not performance.

Not spiritual severity.

It is the heart resting in the Beloved.

It is knowledge becoming love.

It is practice becoming trust.

It is the whole inner life
turning toward the One
who never loses us.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“No sincere step is lost”

Breathing out:
“I am held on the way”
