## 56 — The Eternal Spark

> *The soul carries within it a spark of the Eternal.  
> Beneath all confusion, the heart longs to return to love.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke
of two conditions
of the soul.

There are those
who wander
within the changing world—
caught in fear,
desire,
forgetfulness,
and impermanence.

And there are those
who awaken
into a deeper reality,
beyond the restless currents
of illusion.

Yet beyond both,
Krishna said,
is the Supreme Presence itself.

The One
who sustains all worlds.

The One
who enters all realms
without being bound by them.

The source
beneath every search.

The life
within every life.

The Eternal Person
beyond all passing forms.

Krishna said:

Those who see clearly
recognize this Presence
with their whole being.

Not merely with the intellect,
but with the heart.

And in this recognition,
their lives become whole.

For beneath all human striving
there has always been
a deeper longing.

Not merely for pleasure.

Not merely for success.

Not merely for escape from pain.

But for union
with what is eternal.

Krishna called this teaching
a great secret.

Not because it is hidden
through cruelty,
but because the restless ego
rarely grows quiet enough
to receive it.

The soul wanders
through countless distractions,
forgetting its deeper nature.

Yet beneath the wandering,
the eternal spark remains.

Always longing
for its true source.

### Along the Way

This passage speaks
to one of the deepest tensions
within human life.

Part of us is fragile,
reactive,
and easily lost.

We become tangled
in comparison,
fear,
ego,
and endless craving.

We forget who we are.

We search for permanence
inside temporary things.

But Krishna also insists
that something eternal
lives within us.

A deeper essence
that does not disappear
beneath confusion.

This idea carries
profound dignity.

Human beings are not described
as worthless,
nor merely mechanical,
nor abandoned
inside a meaningless universe.

The soul is portrayed
as carrying
a spark of the sacred itself.

And yet,
we are also reminded
that the ego
is not ultimate.

The spiritual life
is not about becoming grandiose
or self-worshipping.

It is about remembering
our relationship
to something infinitely greater
than ourselves.

Modern life often teaches
that fulfillment comes
through accumulation.

More achievement.

More stimulation.

More consumption.

But even after obtaining
what we once desired,
the ache often remains.

Krishna points toward
a deeper hunger.

The longing
to reconnect
with the source of life itself.

This longing appears
in many quiet ways:

the desire for peace,
the ache for unconditional love,
the yearning for wholeness,
the search for truth,
the longing to finally rest
from endless striving.

The “great secret”
is perhaps simpler
than we imagine.

That the soul
does not ultimately find freedom
through domination,
status,
or possession.

It finds freedom
through awakening.

Through surrendering
the illusion
that the isolated ego
is enough.

And through learning,
little by little,
to live from love
instead of fear.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“There is a deeper light within me”

Breathing out:
“Lead me home to love”
