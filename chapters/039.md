## 39 - The Terror of the Infinite

> *The Holy is not only gentle light. It is also the power before which every illusion trembles.*

There are moments when reality no longer feels soft or manageable. The heart catches sight of something immense — not merely beautiful, but overwhelming. A truth so vast that the ordinary structures of the self begin shaking beneath it.

Most people spend much of life trying to avoid such moments.

We prefer what feels familiar. Contained. Predictable. We want the sacred to comfort us without undoing us. We want mystery that leaves our illusions mostly intact. Yet the deeper spiritual traditions speak honestly: there are encounters with truth that expose how fragile our certainties really are.

And when this happens, the soul trembles.

There is a kind of awe that does not merely soothe. It strips away pretense. A person suddenly sees the passing nature of all things — youth fading, power dissolving, achievements turning to dust, entire civilizations rising and disappearing like waves upon the sea. The mind resists this vision at first because the ego survives by pretending permanence can be secured.

But reality keeps speaking otherwise. Everything changes. Everything passes through the hands of time.

This can feel terrifying when seen clearly. Not because life loses meaning, but because false foundations begin crumbling. The identities we cling to so tightly reveal themselves to be temporary garments. Even the conflicts that consume us begin appearing strangely small beneath the vastness of existence.

And yet hidden within this terror there is also mercy.

For the soul grows exhausted trying to preserve what cannot ultimately be preserved. Much of human suffering comes from resisting impermanence — grasping for certainty where none can fully exist. We tighten against change. We build identities around fragile things. We imagine ourselves separate from the great movement of birth and loss unfolding through all creation.

But contemplation slowly teaches surrender. Not passive resignation. Not indifference.

Something deeper.

The willingness to stand honestly before reality without fleeing into denial.

The ancient teachers understood that wisdom begins when illusion loosens its grip. Not when fear disappears completely, but when the heart becomes willing to remain present even while trembling. There are truths too large to absorb calmly. Mortality is one of them. Vastness is another. The soul sometimes shakes because it is standing before mystery without its usual defenses.

And still, the sacred does not turn away from human fear.

Again and again across the wisdom traditions, when human beings encounter overwhelming mystery, they cry out for mercy. Not polished prayers. Not carefully constructed theology. Simply the raw honesty of the heart recognizing its own smallness.

Hold me. Stay near me. Do not abandon me in this vastness.

Perhaps this too is holy.

To stop pretending invulnerability. To let awe humble us into sincerity. To discover that beneath all our striving for control, the deepest prayer may simply be the willingness to stand exposed before truth — trembling, finite, and still somehow held within a mystery greater than fear itself.

### Breath Prayer

> Inhale: *Hold me in truth*
>
> Exhale: *Have mercy on my fear*
