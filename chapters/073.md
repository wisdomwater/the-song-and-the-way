## 73 - Come Home to Love

> *Beneath all striving, the soul has always been searching for the safety of Divine Love.*

There comes a moment in many spiritual journeys when the heart grows tired of carrying itself alone. The endless effort to prove worthiness, secure certainty, maintain control, or construct a flawless identity begins to feel unbearably heavy. Even sincere spiritual seeking can become exhausting when it is driven mostly by fear.

The soul quietly asks:

Is there anywhere I can finally rest?

The deepest wisdom traditions answer this question not with condemnation, but with invitation. Beneath all the striving, there remains a Presence calling gently to the weary heart. Not demanding perfection before love is offered. Not asking the soul to earn belonging through endless performance. But inviting surrender into something already waiting with open arms.

A surrender not into fear,

but into love.

Human beings cling tightly to many identities. Success. Failure. Religious certainty. Reputation. Roles carefully built over years of effort. We try to save ourselves through achievement, morality, intellect, approval, or control. Yet none of these things can fully carry the weight of the soul’s deepest longing.

Because beneath every lesser attachment there remains a more ancient desire:

To be known completely and still embraced.

To stop hiding.

To stop performing.

To stop carrying life as though everything depends entirely upon the isolated self.

This is why surrender becomes healing rather than frightening when love is finally trusted. The heart loosens not because it is forced into submission, but because it discovers it no longer needs to defend itself endlessly. The soul begins resting inside a Presence that feels more intimate than fear and more enduring than shame.

Something softens there.

Prayer changes too. It becomes less about managing the sacred and more about communion. Less about perfect language and more about honest nearness. The person no longer approaches the Divine merely as distant authority, but as living presence — companion, beloved, quiet refuge beneath all the noise of the world.

And slowly, another kind of life begins unfolding inwardly.

The anxious need to justify oneself weakens.

The fear of rejection loosens.

Love becomes less possessive and more spacious because the soul is no longer starving for worth. Compassion deepens naturally when the heart feels held by something greater than its own fragile striving.

Perhaps this is why the final movement of genuine spirituality is often tenderness rather than intensity. After all the effort, all the searching, all the questions and disciplines, the soul finally discovers what it has always longed for beneath every restless pursuit:

Not domination.

Not perfection.

But home.

A place where love is no longer something to earn,

only something to awaken within.

### Breath Prayer

> Inhale: *I release my fear*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in Divine Love*
