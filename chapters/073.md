## 73 — Come Home to Love

> *The final word of the Gita is not fear. It is invitation.*

## The Song

Krishna said:

Leave behind
every lesser identity
you cling to.

Every role
you think
must save you.

Every anxious attempt
to justify yourself.

Every burden
you were never meant
to carry alone.

Come to Me.

Not halfway.

Not cautiously.

Not while still
keeping one hand
on your illusion of control.

Come fully.

Take refuge in Me alone.

And do not be afraid.

I will free you
from every chain
that binds your heart.

I will carry you
through every shadow.

I will deliver you
from all that separates you
from Love.

Do not despair.

Then Krishna reveals
what has always been true.

The soul’s deepest home
is not found
in achievement.

Not in status.

Not in power.

Not even in religious certainty.

The soul was made
for communion.

For intimacy.

For Divine Love.

And beyond every world
of striving,
beyond every cycle
of fear and becoming,
there is a realm
where Love itself
is the atmosphere.

A place
where the Divine
is not distant.

Where beauty
is alive.

Where every tree,
every river,
every field
sings with presence.

Where Krishna
moves among His beloved
not as an unreachable ruler,
but as companion,
friend,
beloved.

The flute still sounds there.

The heart still remembers.

And those whose eyes
have been softened
by love
begin even now
to glimpse that kingdom
within themselves.

### Along the Way

This verse —
“Abandon all dharmas
and surrender unto Me alone” —
has often been used
to build systems
of fear,
submission,
and spiritual control.

But read carefully.

Krishna is not asking Arjuna
to abandon compassion,
wisdom,
or love.

He is asking him
to release
every false structure
that keeps him
from trusting
the Divine directly.

Sometimes religion itself
becomes one of those structures.

The ego can hide
inside morality.

Inside doctrine.

Inside performance.

Inside the exhausting effort
to prove
we are worthy.

But Divine Love
cannot be earned
through anxiety.

The invitation here
is radical trust.

A surrender
not into fear,
but into relationship.

And notice:
the promise comes
before perfection.

“I will deliver you.”

Not:
“Deliver yourself.”

The movement
toward God
begins not
with flawless spirituality,
but with willingness.

With openness.

With turning the heart
toward Love itself.

The mystical traditions
across the world
often point toward
this same mystery:

At the deepest level,
the soul does not awaken
through domination.

It awakens
through union.

Through participation
in Divine Life.

And perhaps
the true meaning
of surrender
is simply this:

To stop resisting
the Love
that has always
been calling us home.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“I release my fear”

Breathing out:
“I rest in Divine Love”
