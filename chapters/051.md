## 51 — Beyond the Pull of the Inner Currents

> *The soul suffers when it is unconsciously carried by its inner currents.  
> Freedom begins when awareness grows stronger than compulsion.*

## The Song

Krishna continued speaking
of the three movements
that shape the human heart.

When darkness grows strong,
the soul becomes clouded.

Clarity fades.

Energy weakens.

Confusion settles heavily
over the mind.

One drifts through life
half-awake,
unable to see clearly,
unable to move with purpose.

This is the weight
of ignorance.

But when goodness fills the heart,
light begins to spread inwardly.

Awareness sharpens.

Wisdom deepens.

The soul becomes lighter,
more peaceful,
more capable
of seeing truth.

And when passion dominates,
restlessness awakens.

Desire multiplies.

Ambition surges.

The mind races endlessly
toward achievement,
recognition,
and possession.

Krishna said
these inner currents
shape not only life,
but the direction
of the soul itself.

What we continually nourish
becomes the atmosphere
of our inner world.

Goodness gives rise
to wisdom and purity.

Passion gives rise
to greed,
striving,
and dissatisfaction.

Ignorance gives rise
to delusion,
confusion,
and spiritual sleep.

Some rise toward greater light.

Some remain trapped
in endless striving.

Some sink downward
into deeper unconsciousness.

But Krishna spoke
of a freedom
beyond all three.

The awakened soul
learns to observe
these movements
without becoming enslaved by them.

It begins to recognize:

“This restlessness is moving through me.”

“This heaviness is pulling at me.”

“This clarity is present now.”

And through this seeing,
the soul slowly loosens
its identification
with every passing state.

The currents remain,
but they no longer rule completely.

The heart discovers
a deeper center.

A stillness
beyond confusion,
beyond craving,
beyond fear.

And Krishna promised:

The one who rises
beyond the forces
that bind the soul
tastes immortality.

A freedom untouched
by birth,
aging,
loss,
or death.

### Along the Way

This teaching asks us
to become honest observers
of our inner life.

Most people move through the day
without noticing
what is shaping them.

But the currents Krishna describes
are active constantly.

Sometimes we are pulled upward
toward clarity,
peace,
compassion,
and wisdom.

Sometimes outward
into busyness,
comparison,
competition,
and endless striving.

And sometimes downward
into numbness,
avoidance,
exhaustion,
or despair.

None of these states
are entirely permanent.

They rise and fall
like weather patterns
within the heart.

Suffering increases
when we completely identify
with them.

When we believe:
“This anxiety is who I am.”
“This ambition defines me.”
“This heaviness will last forever.”

But awareness creates freedom.

The moment we can gently observe
what is happening within us,
we are no longer fully trapped inside it.

This is why contemplative practice matters.

Silence.

Prayer.

Reflection.

Stillness.

These practices strengthen
the observing awareness
that can notice
without immediately reacting.

Over time,
we begin to see
how easily the ego
becomes addicted
to restlessness and stimulation.

Modern life often rewards
the energy of passion:
constant productivity,
constant motion,
constant striving.

But a soul driven endlessly
by craving
rarely finds peace.

Likewise,
ignorance is not always dramatic evil.

Sometimes it appears quietly
as chronic distraction,
emotional avoidance,
or the slow deadening
of wonder and compassion.

Krishna’s invitation
is not merely
to become “better behaved.”

It is to awaken.

To recognize
the forces moving within us
and gradually stop living
as their unconscious servant.

Beyond the inner storms,
there is a deeper stillness.

A deeper life.

And the soul
was made for that freedom.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Awaken me from confusion”

Breathing out:
“Lead me into deeper freedom”
