## 50 — The Currents That Shape the Heart

> *The soul is pulled by unseen inner currents.  
> Awareness begins when we learn to recognize their movement within us.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke
of the hidden forces
that move through human life.

Three great currents
flow through the world,
shaping thought,
desire,
action,
and perception.

From these currents
the moods of humanity arise.

Clarity.

Restlessness.

Confusion.

He said that all living beings
enter the womb of material life,
and from that vast mystery
countless forms emerge.

Every creature.

Every body.

Every life.

And once born into the world,
the soul becomes bound
by the qualities of nature.

Sometimes
the heart is filled with light.

Clear seeing.

Peace.

Gentleness.

A quiet happiness
untouched by excess craving.

This is the movement
of goodness.

It lifts the soul upward
toward wisdom and balance.

Yet even goodness
can become attachment—
the subtle pride
of needing to feel peaceful,
wise,
or spiritually accomplished.

Then another current rises.

Passion.

The fire of wanting.

Ambition.

Restlessness.

The constant drive
to achieve,
possess,
be noticed,
become more.

The mind races.

The body strains.

The heart attaches itself
to outcomes,
recognition,
control.

Action multiplies,
but peace grows distant.

And beneath both
lies another movement.

Darkness.

Confusion.

Heaviness.

Forgetfulness.

The soul loses clarity
and drifts into numbness,
avoidance,
laziness,
or despair.

The inner light grows dim.

Life becomes clouded.

Krishna said
these three forces
continually struggle
within human beings.

One rises.

Another falls.

Then the balance shifts again.

Like changing weather
moving across the sky of the heart.

The wise learn
to notice these movements.

Not with condemnation,
but with awareness.

For freedom begins
when we stop being unconsciously ruled
by every passing current.

### Along the Way

This teaching is deeply psychological.

Krishna describes
the human condition
with remarkable honesty.

Most people are not guided
by stable wisdom alone.

We are shaped constantly
by inner energies
that rise and fall within us.

Sometimes we feel clear,
compassionate,
and grounded.

Other times restless,
driven,
unable to stop striving.

And sometimes
we collapse inward—
numb,
disconnected,
without energy or direction.

These movements
are part of being human.

The problem begins
when we become completely identified
with whichever state
currently holds us.

Goodness brings clarity,
but even goodness
can quietly feed the ego
if we become attached
to appearing wise or spiritual.

Passion fuels achievement,
but unchecked,
it exhausts the soul.

Many modern lives
are dominated by this energy:
constant productivity,
constant stimulation,
constant striving.

The body remains busy,
but the heart rarely rests.

Ignorance is not merely
lack of information.

It is spiritual sleep.

The dulling of awareness.

The slow loss
of inward aliveness.

It appears whenever we avoid truth,
numb ourselves endlessly,
or stop paying attention
to the deeper movements
of the soul.

Krishna’s wisdom
is not meant to shame us.

It is meant to awaken us.

The first step toward freedom
is learning to recognize
what is moving within us.

To notice:
“This restlessness is rising.”
“This heaviness is pulling at me.”
“This clarity feels clean and spacious.”

Awareness creates space.

And in that space,
the soul slowly becomes
less reactive,
less controlled,
less enslaved
to every passing mood.

We begin to choose
rather than merely drift.

And over time,
the heart learns
how to move toward light
without becoming attached
even to the light itself.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Help me see clearly”

Breathing out:
“Guide my heart toward light”
