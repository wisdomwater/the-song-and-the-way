## 38 - When the Veil Falls Away

> *The soul longs to see the Infinite. Yet true vastness can undo us.*

There are moments in life when ordinary understanding suddenly feels too small. The mind reaches toward something greater — some deeper reality beneath appearances — and the heart begins longing not merely to believe, but to see.

Not with the eyes alone.

With the whole being.

Many people quietly carry this longing. They want certainty strong enough to dissolve every doubt. An experience so luminous it rearranges the inner world forever. A glimpse beyond the veil of ordinary perception into the deeper mystery holding all things together.

And yet the wisdom traditions often speak carefully here. For encounters with vastness are not always comfortable.

Sometimes they undo us.

A person stands beneath an endless night sky and suddenly feels both tiny and strangely free. Someone sits in deep silence long enough for the usual noise of the self to fall away, and for a moment everything feels immeasurably alive. There are experiences of beauty, grief, awe, or stillness that loosen the familiar boundaries through which we organize reality.

And when this happens, the ego trembles.

Because the self prefers a manageable world. Predictable. Contained. Small enough to control. But genuine encounters with mystery reveal how little can actually be possessed by the mind. The soul glimpses something immense moving beneath all appearances, and ordinary certainty begins dissolving.

This can feel beautiful.

And frightening.

For there is a kind of sacred awe that humbles every human being who truly enters it. Not humiliation, but the sudden recognition that life is infinitely larger than our private narratives. The burden of needing to stand at the center of existence begins loosening. Something older and deeper than the isolated self becomes visible for a moment.

The ancient teachers often spoke of this as another kind of seeing. Not merely intellectual understanding, but awakened perception. A way of beholding reality shaped by humility, surrender, attentiveness, and grace. The deepest truths cannot always be forced into language because they are encountered before they are explained.

The mind falls silent.

The heart bows instinctively.

And mystery remains mystery.

Yet there is tenderness here too. The Infinite is not portrayed as cold distance or impersonal emptiness. The sacred draws near even while remaining immeasurable. The same Presence that stretches beyond stars also meets the human soul intimately — through quiet guidance, through mercy, through companionship along the inward path.

This paradox changes a person slowly. The soul begins realizing that every life rests within something far greater than it can fully comprehend. Control loosens. Defensiveness softens. Wonder returns. And gradually, humility ceases feeling like diminishment and begins feeling like freedom.

Perhaps this is part of spiritual awakening: not mastering mystery, but learning how to live reverently within it. To let the veil fall away just enough for the heart to remember that beneath all things — beauty and terror, silence and movement, life and death — there remains a Presence too vast to contain, and yet near enough to breathe within us still.

### Breath Prayer

> Inhale: *Open my inner sight*
>
> Exhale: *Teach me holy wonder*
