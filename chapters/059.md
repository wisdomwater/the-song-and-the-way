## 59 — The Three Gates

> *Lust, anger, and greed slowly hollow out the soul.  
> Freedom begins when the heart learns another way to live.*

## The Song

Krishna continued
speaking of the heart
lost in illusion.

The mind becomes crowded
with endless thoughts.

Restless desires.

Anxieties without end.

The soul,
ensnared in craving,
falls deeper into confusion.

Some perform outward acts
of spirituality,
yet their hearts remain
filled with pride,
power,
and self-importance.

Their devotion
becomes performance.

Their rituals
become displays of ego.

And so they lose sight
of the sacred Presence
living quietly
within themselves
and within others.

Krishna spoke sorrowfully
of what happens
when the heart hardens this way.

The soul drifts farther
from truth.

Farther from compassion.

Farther from peace.

Not because it is abandoned,
but because it continually chooses
what diminishes it.

Then Krishna named
three great gates
through which darkness enters:

Lust.

Anger.

Greed.

These,
He said,
destroy the soul’s clarity.

They consume the heart
from within.

Lust that endlessly grasps.

Anger that burns
everything it touches.

Greed that never says,
“Enough.”

But Krishna did not speak
only of danger.

He also spoke
of freedom.

Those who turn away
from these paths of darkness
begin to move
toward what is life-giving.

Toward wisdom.

Toward peace.

Toward the highest good.

The soul slowly learns again
how to live
in harmony
with truth.

### Along the Way

This passage feels less
like condemnation
and more like diagnosis.

Krishna is describing
patterns of consciousness
that distort the human heart.

Lust,
anger,
and greed
are not merely moral failures.

They are forces
that cloud perception.

They pull the soul outward,
away from presence,
away from compassion,
away from inner freedom.

Lust is not only sexual desire.

It is the compulsive hunger
that believes fulfillment
always lies in obtaining more.

More pleasure.

More validation.

More control.

Anger,
when left unexamined,
narrows the soul.

It reduces other human beings
into enemies,
obstacles,
or projections
of our pain.

Greed convinces us
that accumulation
will finally quiet our fear.

Yet the more we cling,
the more anxious
we often become.

These movements
feed one another.

Unfulfilled desire
becomes anger.

Anger seeks control.

Control feeds greed.

And soon the heart
becomes exhausted,
reactive,
and spiritually numb.

Krishna’s warning
is deeply compassionate
because it invites awareness.

We cannot heal
what we refuse to see.

Many people carry shame
around these struggles,
but shame alone
rarely transforms the heart.

Honest awareness does.

Gentle honesty.

The courage to notice
what rules us.

The courage to ask:

What desires
are shaping my life?

What fears
are feeding my anger?

What emptiness
am I trying to fill?

The spiritual life
is not the destruction
of desire itself.

It is the transformation
of desire.

A movement away
from compulsive grasping
and toward love,
wisdom,
and inner freedom.

Little by little,
the soul learns
another way to live.

Not driven constantly
by craving.

Not ruled by resentment.

Not consumed by accumulation.

But rooted instead
in presence,
contentment,
and compassion.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Free me from restless craving”

Breathing out:
“Lead me into peace”
