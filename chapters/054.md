## 54 — The Root Above

> *The soul becomes free when it loosens its grip on illusion.  
> Beneath all longing is a deeper longing for home.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke again
of the great tree
of worldly existence.

Its roots hidden above.

Its branches spreading downward
through the tangled landscapes
of human life.

No one can fully trace
where this tree begins.

Its pathways twist endlessly.

Desire feeding desire.

Fear feeding fear.

One attachment giving birth
to another.

The soul wanders
through its branches,
searching for permanence
among temporary things.

So Krishna said:

Take up
the strong blade
of detachment.

Cut through illusion.

Cut through the endless belief
that possessions,
status,
pleasure,
or control
can finally satisfy the heart.

And then—
search for the deeper realm.

The place
from which one does not return
to confusion and wandering.

The eternal source
from which all life
first flowed.

Krishna described
the ones who find this path.

Those who loosen pride.

Those no longer intoxicated
by illusion.

Those who step back
from compulsive craving.

Those who are no longer thrown endlessly
between pleasure and pain.

Such souls,
quiet and awake,
move toward
the imperishable reality.

A realm beyond decay.

A light
not dependent
on sun,
moon,
or fire.

A radiance
that shines from within itself.

And Krishna said:

The living beings
are eternal sparks
of the Divine.

Yet they struggle
within the world,
pulled constantly
by the senses
and the restless mind.

The soul moves
from body to body,
carrying impressions,
desires,
memories,
and tendencies—
as wind carries fragrance
from one place to another.

Still,
through all its wandering,
something eternal remains.

Something seeking
its true home.

### Along the Way

This passage speaks
to the deep exhaustion
many people quietly feel.

The world trains us
to chase endlessly.

More success.

More pleasure.

More validation.

More certainty.

Yet even after achieving
what we once desired,
something within us
often remains unsatisfied.

Krishna suggests
that this dissatisfaction
is not accidental.

The soul was never meant
to find its ultimate fulfillment
in temporary things.

The “blade of detachment”
is not hatred toward life.

It is clarity.

The ability to recognize
what cannot permanently sustain us.

Without detachment,
the heart becomes trapped
inside cycles of craving.

Always reaching.

Always anxious.

Always fearing loss.

But detachment loosens
the grip of possession.

We begin to enjoy life
without demanding
that it save us.

Relationships become freer.

Work becomes less consuming.

Success becomes less intoxicating.

Failure becomes less crushing.

The soul gains room to breathe.

Krishna also describes
human beings
as eternal sparks
struggling within material life.

This image is deeply compassionate.

Most people know
what it feels like
to feel divided inside.

Part of us longs for goodness,
peace,
and truth.

Another part remains entangled
in fear,
impulse,
ego,
and distraction.

The spiritual journey
is not about hating this struggle.

It is about becoming conscious of it.

Patient with it.

Awake within it.

And beneath all wandering,
the soul carries
a quiet memory
of something deeper.

A longing
for wholeness.

For rest.

For home.

Not merely a place,
but a way of being—
free from illusion,
free from endless grasping,
free enough at last
to love without fear.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Cut through my illusions”

Breathing out:
“Lead me toward what is eternal”
