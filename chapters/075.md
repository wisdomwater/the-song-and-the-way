## 75 — The Song Ends, But the Journey Begins

> *True wisdom is not merely understood. It is lived.*

## The Song

Sañjaya said:

As I remember
this sacred conversation
between Krishna
and Arjuna,
my heart overflows again
and again.

The words still echo
inside me.

The beauty of it.

The tenderness of it.

The power of it.

And when I remember
the radiant presence
of Krishna Himself,
wonder rises within me
like a great wave.

Again and again
I am filled
with awe.

For wherever
the Divine Presence dwells,
and wherever
a sincere heart
is willing to listen,
there also will be:

wisdom,

courage,

peace,

abundance,

and the victory
of what is true.

And so the Song ends.

The battlefield remains.

The world remains wounded.

The struggle of the human heart
continues.

Yet something
has changed.

Arjuna remembers.

The soul awakens.

And life itself
becomes the path.

### Along the Way

The ending of the Gita
is remarkably human.

There is no dramatic escape
from the world.

No dissolving into light.

No abandoning
ordinary life.

Arjuna still must walk
into uncertainty.

Still must act.

Still must live.

But now
he does so awake.

This may be
one of the deepest truths
of the spiritual life:

Awakening does not remove us
from human existence.

It transforms
how we move through it.

The Gita begins
with collapse.

Fear.

Confusion.

Grief.

Arjuna cannot move forward.

By the end,
the outer world
has not changed much.

But the inner world has.

And because
the inner world changes,
everything else
can now be faced differently.

This is why
the Gita continues
to matter across centuries.

The battlefield
is not only ancient.

It is inward.

Every person
eventually stands
between fear and love,
between despair and trust,
between ego and surrender,
between illusion and awakening.

And the voice
that speaks to Arjuna
still speaks quietly
within the human soul:

Remember who you are.

Act with love.

Release your fear.

Do your work.

Trust the Divine.

Walk forward.

The final image
is beautiful.

Where Krishna is,
and where the sincere seeker stands beside Him,
there is righteousness,
wisdom,
and victory.

Not victory
as domination.

But the victory
of awakening over fear.

The victory
of love over separation.

The victory
of truth
over illusion.

And perhaps
that is the real ending
of the Gita:

Not a closed book.

But an invitation
to live differently.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“I awaken to Love”

Breathing out:
“I walk the path in peace”
