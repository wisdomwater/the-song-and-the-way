## 72 — The Place Where Surrender Becomes Peace

> *The deepest spiritual invitation is not control, but trust.*

## The Song

Krishna said:

Through love,
the soul
comes to truly know Me.

Not merely through ritual.

Not merely through philosophy.

Not through fear.

But through devotion.

And when one truly knows Me,
one enters
into My life.

Even while carrying out
ordinary responsibilities,
the one who takes shelter in Me
reaches the eternal.

So Krishna tells Arjuna:

Offer everything to Me.

Your actions.

Your confusion.

Your victories.

Your failures.

Your fears.

Your future.

Do your work,
but do not carry
the unbearable burden
of believing
you are alone.

Keep your mind
resting in Me.

And by grace,
you will cross
through every difficulty.

But if,
through pride,
you refuse this wisdom,
you will remain lost
inside your own illusion.

For even now,
Krishna says,
your deeper nature
is already calling you.

You cannot escape
what your soul
was created for.

The Divine dwells
within the heart
of every living being.

Quietly guiding.

Quietly calling.

Quietly turning
the great wheel of life.

So surrender,
Krishna says,
with your whole heart.

And there
you will discover
a peace
that does not disappear.

Then Krishna grows softer.

More intimate.

More personal.

You are dear to Me,
He says.

So listen carefully.

Fix your mind on Me.

Love Me.

Walk with Me.

Honor the sacred.

And you will come home
to Me.

This is My promise.

### Along the Way

There is something
deeply tender
about this section.

The Gita moves here
from philosophy
into relationship.

The invitation
is no longer merely:
“Understand reality.”

It becomes:

“Trust Love itself.”

Many spiritual paths
begin with discipline.

With study.

With purification.

With effort.

But eventually,
every authentic path
arrives at surrender.

Not passive resignation.

Not helpless collapse.

But the releasing
of the isolated self
that believes
everything depends
upon its own control.

Krishna tells Arjuna
that the Divine
already dwells
within him.

Already guides him.

Already knows him.

Already loves him.

This changes
the spiritual journey entirely.

The goal is no longer
to earn Divine acceptance.

The invitation
is to awaken
to a Presence
that has always
been present.

And perhaps
the most healing line
is this:

“You are dear to Me.”

So many people
carry an image of God
rooted in fear,
performance,
or disappointment.

But here,
the Divine speaks
with affection.

With closeness.

With intimacy.

Not:
“Prove yourself worthy.”

But:
“Come near.”

The soul begins healing
when it realizes
it is not merely tolerated
by the Divine,
but deeply loved.

And from that love,
surrender becomes possible.

Not because we are coerced.

But because love
finally feels safe enough
to trust.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“I release my striving”

Breathing out:
“I rest in Divine love”
