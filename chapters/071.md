## 71 — The Quiet Work of Becoming Whole

> *Transformation rarely happens through force. It happens through steady surrender.*

## The Song

Krishna told Arjuna:

Do not abandon
the work
that belongs to your life.

Every path
contains imperfection.

Every fire
carries smoke.

Still—
walk the path
that is truly yours.

Not the life
performed for approval.

Not the identity
borrowed from others.

Not the role
driven by fear.

But the work
your soul
has been shaped for.

And as one walks,
Krishna says,
something deeper unfolds.

The mind grows clearer.

Desires loosen.

Attachment softens.

The need to possess,
control,
or dominate
begins to fade.

One becomes quieter.

More inwardly spacious.

Less hungry
for constant stimulation.

The senses
no longer rule the soul.

A person begins
to delight
in simplicity.

In stillness.

In contemplation.

In truth.

And gradually,
the hardened self
begins to dissolve.

The ego loosens
its grip.

Anger softens.

Pride weakens.

The anxious need
to prove oneself
falls away.

A deeper peace emerges.

And then,
Krishna says,
a sacred shift occurs.

The soul no longer
lives trapped
between craving
and grief.

No longer endlessly grasping.

No longer endlessly mourning.

One begins to see
all beings
with equal vision.

And from this awakening
love itself
is born.

### Along the Way

This section feels less
like a command
and more like
a description
of spiritual maturation.

Krishna is not describing
perfection overnight.

He is describing
the slow reshaping
of consciousness.

And that matters.

Because many people
approach spirituality
as another form
of self-improvement anxiety.

Become purer.

Become detached.

Become enlightened.

Become disciplined.

Become holy.

But transformation
cannot be forced
through self-hatred.

The soul does not heal
through violence against itself.

Krishna instead describes
a gradual reordering
of desire.

As awareness deepens,
certain things
simply lose their grip.

Not because we suppress them,
but because we begin tasting
something more nourishing.

A quieter joy.

A steadier love.

A deeper freedom.

This is why
the spiritual life
often includes practices
that initially feel difficult:

Silence.

Restraint.

Meditation.

Honest reflection.

Compassion.

Forgiveness.

Simplicity.

At first,
the ego resists them.

But over time,
they create space
for another kind of life
to emerge.

And perhaps one of the most
beautiful lines here
is this:

“The awakened person
neither rejoices
nor laments.”

This does not mean
becoming emotionally numb.

It means no longer
being enslaved
by circumstance.

The heart becomes steadier.

Less controlled
by gain and loss.

Less shattered
by every passing wave.

The soul begins
to rest
in something deeper
than temporary conditions.

And from that place,
love flows more freely.

Not possessive love.

Not anxious love.

But spacious love.

The kind that sees
the sacred
within all beings.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Quiet the striving within me”

Breathing out:
“Lead me into deeper peace”
