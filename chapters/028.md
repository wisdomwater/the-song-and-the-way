## 28 — The One Who Knows Us

> *We are born into longing and resistance,  
> yet the Eternal remains nearer than our confusion.*

## The Song

Krishna continued speaking
of the mystery hidden from restless eyes.

The fruits gained by lesser desires,
He said,
do not last.

What is sought for the sake of craving
eventually fades.

Those who worship passing powers
reach what is passing.

But those whose hearts belong to Me
come to Me.

Still,
many do not understand My nature.

They imagine the Eternal
as something that briefly takes form,
as though the deathless could be reduced
to a passing appearance.

They do not see
the imperishable mystery.

They do not recognize
the unborn One.

Krishna said:

I am not revealed to everyone.

I am hidden by My own sacred power.

The world, bewildered,
does not know Me
as eternal and without birth.

Yet I know all beings.

I know what has been.

I know what is.

I know what will come.

Nothing is hidden from Me.

But beings do not know Me,
because from the beginning
they are born into duality.

Desire and aversion
rise within them.

I want this.

I reject that.

I love what pleases me.

I hate what threatens me.

And caught between these movements,
the heart becomes confused.

It mistakes preference for truth.

It mistakes fear for wisdom.

It mistakes craving for life.

But those whose lives are purified
begin to move beyond this divided seeing.

Freed from the illusion
of desire and hatred,
they turn toward the Eternal
with steadiness.

They take refuge
because they long to be free
from the old fear of decay and death.

And in that refuge,
they come to know
the Absolute,
the self,
and the deep law of action.

They learn to see
matter,
the heavenly powers,
and every offering
as held within the One.

And when the final hour comes,
those whose minds have been gathered
in this knowing
do not lose the Beloved.

Even at death,
they remember.

Even in the great crossing,
they are known.

Then Arjuna,
listening with reverence,
asked the questions
that arise when the soul stands
before ultimate things:

What is the Absolute?

What is the self?

What is karma?

What is this world of becoming?

What are the powers beyond us?

What is sacrifice?

And how can the Eternal be known
at the time of death?

### Along the Way

This passage speaks with unusual honesty
about why spiritual sight becomes difficult.

It is not simply that truth is far away.

It is that the heart is often divided.

Desire pulls one direction.

Aversion pulls another.

We spend so much of life
chasing what pleases us
and fleeing what wounds us
that we begin to lose the capacity
to see clearly.

This is not only a religious problem.

It is deeply human.

We interpret life through attraction and fear.

We call something good
because it gives us comfort.

We call something bad
because it threatens our plans.

We cling to what affirms the self
we have built.

We reject what exposes it.

And slowly,
the world becomes a mirror
of our cravings.

The Gita invites us beyond this narrow vision.

It does not ask us to become emotionless.

It asks us to become free.

Free enough to notice desire
without worshiping it.

Free enough to feel aversion
without becoming ruled by it.

Free enough to seek what is true
rather than merely what is pleasant.

This freedom grows through refuge.

The passage reminds us
that the Eternal knows us
more fully than we know ourselves.

Past,
present,
future —
all are held in a knowing
deeper than our self-awareness.

This can feel humbling.

But it can also bring comfort.

We are not unseen.

We are not random.

We are not abandoned
inside the confusion of our own hearts.

There is a Presence
that knows the whole story.

And as the chapter turns,
Arjuna begins asking
the great questions.

Not small questions of strategy.

Not questions of victory alone.

Questions of being,
death,
action,
selfhood,
and ultimate reality.

This is the dignity of the human soul:

to ask what matters most.

To stand before mystery
without pretending we already understand.

To let longing become inquiry.

And to seek the One
who can be remembered
even when everything else falls away.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“You know me completely”

Breathing out:
“Lead me beyond fear”
