## 28 - The One Who Knows Us

> *We are born into longing and resistance, yet the Eternal remains nearer than our confusion.*

Much of human suffering begins in division.

The heart reaches toward one thing while fearing another.
The mind clings tightly to what feels pleasurable and recoils from what feels painful.
Desire says, “I must have this.”
Fear says, “I must avoid that.”

And between these two movements, the soul grows weary.

Many people spend their lives pulled back and forth by attraction and aversion without ever noticing how deeply these forces shape perception itself.

We do not always see clearly.

We often see through preference.

What comforts us appears good.
What threatens us appears unbearable.
What flatters the self feels true.
What unsettles the self feels dangerous.

And slowly, without realizing it, we begin building entire identities around protecting our desires and defending against our fears.

The deeper spiritual traditions speak gently but honestly about this condition.

The problem is not that reality hides itself completely.

It is that the heart becomes clouded.

Like water stirred with mud,
the soul loses its capacity to reflect clearly.

Yet beneath all confusion, something deeper remains present.

The Eternal is not absent simply because we cannot perceive clearly.

The Sacred remains nearer than our wandering minds,
nearer than our fear,
nearer even than the self we struggle so hard to protect.

There is profound comfort in this.

Many people secretly fear they are unseen.

Unknown.
Misunderstood.
Lost inside the complexity of their own contradictions.

But the deeper wisdom traditions whisper another possibility:
that we are known completely already.

Not partially.
Not only through our successes.
Not only through the image we present outwardly.

Completely.

The hidden grief.
The divided motives.
The tenderness we conceal.
The wounds beneath our anger.
The longing beneath our striving.

Nothing is hidden from the gaze of Love.

At first this can feel frightening.

To be fully known seems dangerous in a world where vulnerability is often met with judgment or rejection.

But the Sacred does not know us in order to condemn us.

It knows us in order to hold us.

To accompany us patiently through the long unfolding of the soul.

This is why spiritual maturity requires more than merely chasing pleasant experiences.

The heart must slowly become free enough to seek truth even when truth unsettles the ego.

To remain present with discomfort without immediately escaping into distraction.
To notice desire without surrendering to it automatically.
To notice fear without allowing fear to define reality.

This kind of freedom changes the quality of attention itself.

The soul becomes quieter.
More spacious.
More capable of seeing beyond immediate impulse.

And from this deeper place, the great questions begin rising naturally.

Who am I beneath all my roles and defenses?
What remains when possessions, achievements, and appearances fall away?
What is death?
What is truly lasting?
What does it mean to live well?
What carries the soul beyond fear?

These are holy questions.

Not because they are easily answered,
but because they open the heart beyond superficial living.

The soul becomes alive when it dares to stand before mystery honestly.

Not pretending certainty.
Not fleeing discomfort.

Simply remaining open.

And perhaps this openness itself becomes a form of prayer —
a quiet turning toward the One who has already known us from the beginning.

### Breath Prayer

> Inhale: *You know me completely*
>
> Exhale: *Lead me beyond fear*
