## 40 — The Instrument of Time

> *Time devours every kingdom of the ego.  
> Yet even within its fire, the soul is invited to serve.*

## The Song

Arjuna trembled
before the terrible form.

Krishna’s mouths burned
like the fires
at the end of the world.

All directions disappeared.

All certainty dissolved.

The universe itself
seemed to blaze
inside the radiance
of the Eternal.

Arjuna bowed low.

Tell me who You are,
he cried.

This form is fierce.

I cannot understand
what You are doing.

Be merciful to me.

And Krishna answered:

I am Time,
the mighty destroyer of worlds.

I have come
to gather all things back.

Even without you,
these warriors will not remain.

Their end
has already been woven
into the movement of things.

So arise.

Do what has been given to you.

Stand in your place.

Become an instrument.

The battle was no longer
only Arjuna’s burden.

He was not the author
of history.

He was not the keeper
of every outcome.

He was being asked
to participate faithfully
in a movement
larger than himself.

Not from hatred.

Not from pride.

Not from ambition.

But from obedience
to the truth
placed before him.

The names were spoken:

Drona.

Bhishma.

Karna.

The great warriors.

The beloved teachers.

The impossible griefs.

Already held
within the mystery of Time.

Do not be overcome,
Krishna said.

Act.

You will prevail.

And Arjuna,
still shaking,
folded his hands.

His voice faltered.

The one who had argued,
questioned,
resisted,
and grieved
now stood before a truth
too vast to master.

The worlds rejoice in You,
he said.

The wicked flee.

The perfected bow.

For You are beyond
what exists
and what does not exist.

You are the shelter
of the universe.

### Along the Way

This passage brings us
to one of the most sobering moments
in the Gita.

The Divine speaks as Time.

Not time as clocks
or calendars,
but time as the force
that carries all things
toward change,
loss,
death,
and transformation.

Everything within creation
is touched by it.

No empire escapes it.

No body escapes it.

No achievement escapes it.

No conflict remains forever.

For the ego,
this is terrifying.

The ego wants permanence
on its own terms.

It wants control.

It wants to believe
that if it plans well enough,
fights hard enough,
or clings tightly enough,
it can secure itself
against the flow of life.

But Time reveals
the limits of control.

This can crush us
if we meet it without trust.

But it can also free us.

Arjuna is not told
to control history.

He is not told
to carry the whole world
on his shoulders.

He is told
to become an instrument.

This is a difficult
and tender wisdom.

We are responsible
for our participation.

For our motives.

For our courage.

For the love
and clarity
with which we act.

But we are not responsible
for being the source
of all outcomes.

There is a release here.

Not a release
from action,
but a release
from the illusion
that everything depends
upon the isolated self.

To become an instrument
is not to become passive.

It is to become available.

Available to truth.

Available to compassion.

Available to the next faithful step.

Sometimes that step is gentle.

Sometimes it is courageous.

Sometimes it asks us
to face what we would rather avoid.

But the invitation remains:

Act without hatred.

Serve without ego.

Trust without needing
to possess the whole future.

And when the vastness
of life becomes too much,
do what Arjuna does.

Bow.

Tell the truth.

Ask for mercy.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Make me available to truth”

Breathing out:
“Free me from control”
