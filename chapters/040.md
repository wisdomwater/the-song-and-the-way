## 40 - The Instrument of Time

> *Time devours every kingdom of the ego. Yet even within its fire, the soul is invited to serve.*

There are moments when life strips away the illusion that we are fully in control. Plans unravel. Bodies age. Relationships change. Entire seasons of life disappear before we are ready to release them. The future we imagined bends in another direction, and the soul finds itself standing before a truth it cannot negotiate away. Everything moves within the current of time.

This realization can feel frightening at first. Human beings naturally try to secure themselves against uncertainty. We build identities around achievement, stability, certainty, and control. We want to believe that with enough wisdom, effort, or preparation, we can protect ourselves from loss.

But time keeps humbling every illusion of permanence. Empires vanish. Reputations fade. Strength weakens. Even grief itself changes shape with the passing years.

And yet the deeper spiritual traditions do not offer this truth merely to wound the heart. They offer it to free us from carrying burdens that were never ours to hold completely.

Much of human suffering comes from trying to govern what cannot ultimately be governed. We exhaust ourselves imagining we are responsible for every outcome, every future possibility, every unfolding movement of life around us. The ego quietly whispers that everything depends upon us.

But wisdom speaks differently. We are invited to participate faithfully. Not to control the whole river.

There is humility in this realization, but also relief. The soul slowly learns that responsibility and control are not the same thing. We are responsible for the honesty we bring to this moment. For the love with which we speak. For the courage to take the next faithful step placed before us. For whether we act from fear or compassion, from vanity or sincerity.

But the final outcomes of life remain larger than us.

And perhaps this is why surrender matters so deeply on the inward path. Not surrender as passivity or resignation, but surrender as availability. A willingness to become responsive to truth rather than driven entirely by egoic grasping. The heart loosens its demand to possess the future completely and begins listening more carefully for what love requires now.

Sometimes that path is gentle. Sometimes it asks courage. Sometimes it leads directly through grief we would never have chosen.

Still, the invitation remains the same: participate honestly in the life that has been given to you without imagining yourself to be the author of the entire story.

This changes the atmosphere of action itself. A person no longer needs to force reality into constant submission. The soul can work, serve, create, protect, and love without being consumed by the illusion of absolute control. There is effort, but less desperation inside the effort.

And when life becomes too vast to carry alone, the heart can still do what human beings have always done before mystery. Bow honestly. Tell the truth. Ask for mercy.

Perhaps this too is wisdom — to become less obsessed with mastering time, and more willing to become an instrument through which compassion, courage, and truth may briefly move while we are here.

### Breath Prayer
