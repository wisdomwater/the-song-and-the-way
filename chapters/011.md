## 11 — The Ocean That Remains Still

> *The restless mind runs endlessly toward what it believes will complete it.  
> Peace begins when the soul discovers it is already held within something deeper.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke of the senses like untamed horses.

If left unchecked,
they pull the mind endlessly outward.

Toward pleasure.
Toward possession.
Toward distraction.
Toward whatever glitters brightly enough to promise relief from emptiness.

The mind lingers upon a thing.
Then attachment forms.
Attachment becomes craving.
Craving becomes frustration.
Frustration ignites anger.

And anger clouds vision.

Memory falters.
Wisdom weakens.
The self becomes lost inside its own turbulence.

So Krishna called Arjuna inward.

Not into withdrawal from life,
but into mastery within it.

The wise do not become slaves to every impulse that rises within them.

They learn how to hold desire without being consumed by it.

They move among the things of the world without clinging desperately to them.

Pleasure does not intoxicate them.
Pain does not shatter them.

Their peace does not depend upon circumstances remaining favorable.

For the uncontrolled mind is like a small boat seized by violent wind,
dragged helplessly across dark waters.

But the awakened heart becomes steady.

Quiet.

Deep.

Krishna described such a soul as an ocean.

Rivers continually pour into it,
yet the ocean remains full without overflowing.

Desires still arise.
Experiences still move through life.
The world still presses against the senses.

But the one rooted in wisdom is no longer ruled by endless hunger.

They do not build their identity upon possession,
status,
or the fragile performance of self.

They loosen their grip upon “mine.”
They loosen their grip upon “me.”

And in that release,
peace begins to enter.

This way of being seems strange to the world.

What most people chase endlessly,
the sage no longer worships.

What the world overlooks,
the sage treasures quietly within.

While others remain awake chasing distraction,
the inwardly awakened soul keeps watch over something deeper.

And Krishna said:

One who remains rooted in this understanding,
even at the threshold of death,
crosses beyond confusion
and enters lasting freedom.

### Along the Way

The Gita offers here one of its clearest descriptions of how inner suffering grows.

Very rarely does destruction arrive all at once.

It begins with fixation.

The mind circles endlessly around what it wants, fears, envies, or resents. Attention hardens into attachment. Attachment becomes craving. And when craving is obstructed, anger emerges. Soon clarity itself begins to dissolve.

Many people live almost entirely pulled along by this cycle.

Advertising depends on it.
Politics manipulates it.
Entire industries are built around keeping the mind restless, dissatisfied, and externally dependent.

But the spiritually grounded person begins learning another relationship with desire.

Not violent repression.
Not indulgence without wisdom.

Awareness.

The mature soul notices impulses without immediately surrendering to them. It becomes possible to pause before reaction. To observe craving rather than obey it blindly.

This inner steadiness changes everything.

Peace is no longer dependent upon getting exactly what we want.
Identity is no longer constructed entirely around success, pleasure, admiration, or control.

The image of the ocean is important.

A shallow container overflows easily.
A deep ocean can receive countless rivers without losing its center.

So too the awakened heart develops depth.

Experiences still come.
Desires still arise.
Losses still happen.
Pleasures still pass through life.

But they no longer dictate the entire condition of the soul.

This teaching is not anti-human.

It does not ask us to stop loving, enjoying beauty, or participating in life.

It asks something subtler:

Can we live fully without becoming possessed by what we experience?

Can we love without clinging?
Can we act without ego?
Can we receive joy without building our entire identity upon preserving it forever?

The deeper peace described here is not numbness.

It is spaciousness.

The freedom of no longer being dragged helplessly by every passing wave inside the mind.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Deepen my inner stillness”

Breathing out:
“Let peace remain within me”
