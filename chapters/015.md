## 15 — The Fire That Cannot Be Fed

> *What we continually feed will eventually rule us.*

## The Song

Arjuna looked honestly into the human condition.

He had heard Krishna speak of wisdom,
self-control,
clarity,
and peace.

But now another question rose from within him:

“What force drives people toward destruction,
even when they know better?
Why do human beings betray their own conscience?
Why do they act against their deepest knowing,
as though pulled by some hidden power?”

Krishna answered directly.

“It is desire,” He said.

Desire that hardens into grasping.
Desire that becomes obsession.
Desire frustrated until it erupts as anger.

Born from restless passion,
this force consumes endlessly,
never satisfied.

Like fire that devours everything placed into it,
lust and craving always ask for more.

More pleasure.
More control.
More recognition.
More stimulation.
More certainty.
More possession.

And the tragedy is this:

The more it is fed,
the stronger it becomes.

Krishna said that desire clouds consciousness
the way smoke veils fire,
dust covers a mirror,
or the womb conceals the child growing within.

The light remains present,
but it becomes obscured.

So too,
the soul’s clarity becomes hidden beneath compulsions,
attachments,
and endless appetite.

Even the wise can become overcome
when desire infiltrates the senses,
the mind,
and the intellect itself.

The senses pull outward.
The mind becomes fascinated.
The intellect begins justifying what the heart already craves.

And slowly,
discernment weakens.

So Krishna urged Arjuna:

Begin with the senses.

Learn restraint.

Not because the body is evil,
but because unchecked craving eventually enslaves the soul.

The senses are powerful.
The mind is more powerful still.
The intellect stands above the mind.

But deeper than all of them
is the true self —
the witnessing consciousness untouched beneath the turbulence.

Krishna called Arjuna to stand from that deeper place.

To steady the lower self through the higher self.

To stop feeding the inner fire
that could never finally satisfy him.

### Along the Way

One of the most psychologically perceptive teachings in the Gita is this progression:

Attention becomes attachment.  
Attachment becomes craving.  
Craving becomes anger.  
Anger clouds perception.  
Clouded perception destroys wisdom.

Anyone who has watched addiction,
resentment,
envy,
or compulsive behavior unfold can recognize this pattern.

Very few destructive choices begin as outright evil.

Most begin as fixation.

The mind circles something repeatedly.
A fantasy.
A fear.
A wound.
A desire.
An identity.

What we continually rehearse internally slowly gains authority over us.

Modern culture often teaches that freedom means satisfying every impulse.

But the Gita suggests something radically different:

A person ruled by impulse is not free.

Freedom is not the absence of desire.

Freedom is the ability to remain grounded in the presence of desire without becoming enslaved by it.

This teaching reaches far beyond sexuality,
though sexuality is included.

People lust for power,
attention,
certainty,
approval,
status,
comfort,
revenge,
control,
and endless distraction.

The restless appetite of the ego rarely says:
“This is enough.”

And yet,
trying to violently suppress desire also fails.

Suppression alone often turns desire into shame,
secrecy,
or inner fragmentation.

Krishna points toward something deeper than repression:
transformation through awareness and alignment.

The senses are not enemies.

But they are poor masters.

The deeper self —
the conscious witnessing presence —
must learn to guide them wisely.

This requires honesty.

It requires watching the movements of the mind without instantly obeying them.

It requires recognizing that not every craving deserves worship.

And perhaps most importantly,
it requires discovering a deeper nourishment.

Because empty hearts always search for substitutes.

The soul cannot survive forever on stimulation alone.

Eventually,
it longs for something real.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Help me see clearly”

Breathing out:
“Free me from what enslaves me”
