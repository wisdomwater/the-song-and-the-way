## 46 — The Witness Within

> *There is a Presence within you that watches without fear.  
> Beneath all change, something eternal remains awake.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke
of the Light
hidden behind all lights.

The radiance
behind the sun.

The awareness
behind every act
of seeing.

A brilliance
untouched by darkness.

Not merely something
to be believed,
but something
the soul can awaken to.

He said:

This Presence
lives quietly
within every heart.

It is the beginning
of wisdom,
the object of wisdom,
and the fulfillment
of wisdom itself.

To know this deeply
is to move closer
to love.

Closer
to freedom.

Then Krishna turned again
to the mystery
of existence.

Material life
moves constantly.

Bodies are born.

Thoughts rise and fall.

Desires pull the mind
in endless directions.

The world shifts
through pleasure and pain,
gain and loss,
attachment and fear.

The soul,
caught within these movements,
becomes identified
with them.

It clings.

It resists.

It becomes entangled
in the moods
of the world.

And because of this attachment,
it wanders endlessly—
drawn again and again
into cycles
of confusion and longing.

But within the body
there is another Presence.

The Witness.

Silent.

Steady.

Watching every thought,
every action,
every sorrow,
every desire.

The inner Companion
who never abandons us.

The Maintainer.

The quiet guide.

The One
who remains present
even when we forget.

Krishna said:

To truly understand
the difference
between the changing world
and the eternal witness within—
this is liberation.

To awaken
from complete identification
with the restless movements
of the ego.

To remember
what is deeper
than fear,
deeper than craving,
deeper even than death.

### Along the Way

One of the deepest forms
of suffering
comes from total identification
with the changing self.

We believe
we are our emotions.

Our thoughts.

Our failures.

Our status.

Our wounds.

So when these things shake,
we shake with them.

The mind becomes
like water in a storm—
constantly thrown
from one reaction
to another.

But this passage invites us
to discover
another center of awareness.

The witness within.

The quiet presence
capable of observing
without becoming consumed.

This does not mean
becoming emotionally numb
or detached from compassion.

It means learning
to see clearly.

To notice anger
without becoming ruled by it.

To notice fear
without surrendering entirely to it.

To notice desire
without allowing it
to define the soul.

Most people live
completely immersed
in reaction.

Pulled endlessly
by approval,
fear,
comfort,
comparison,
and craving.

But awakening begins
when we pause long enough
to notice:

There is something within us
that can observe all of this.

Something still.

Something spacious.

Something capable
of choosing love
instead of reflex.

Peace
does not come
from controlling every circumstance.

It comes from no longer
being entirely possessed
by every passing circumstance.

And perhaps this is why
silence,
prayer,
reflection,
and contemplation
matter so deeply.

They help us remember
the deeper Presence
that has been quietly there
all along.

Watching.

Holding.

Calling the soul
toward freedom.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Quiet my restless heart”

Breathing out:
“Awaken the deeper light”
