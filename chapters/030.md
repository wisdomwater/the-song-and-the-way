## 30 — Beyond the Returning

> *Everything that rises passes away, yet beneath all endings there remains a deeper Home.*

Everything visible carries within it the quiet mark of impermanence. Morning light fades into evening shadows. Flowers bloom and wither. Children grow into lives of their own. Bodies change slowly beneath the passing years. Entire seasons of life arrive quietly, settle around us for a time, and then disappear before we fully understand they were already leaving.

Even joy changes form. A conversation that once filled a room with laughter becomes only memory. A home once crowded with voices grows still. The moments we wish we could preserve forever continue moving beyond our grasp. And this is one of the deepest sorrows woven into human life: we learn to love what cannot remain exactly as it is.

Because the heart longs for permanence, people often cling tightly to temporary things. Relationships. Identity. Possessions. Accomplishments. Health. We try to secure ourselves against change, hoping that enough control or effort might hold back the movement of time. Yet life continues unfolding beyond our attempts to contain it. The world keeps turning. Seasons keep changing. No amount of fear can stop what was always meant to move.

The ancient wisdom traditions did not speak of impermanence in order to make the soul despair. They spoke of it to loosen the grip of possessiveness. Much suffering grows from demanding eternity from what was only ever meant to be received as a gift for a season. And strangely, when we stop trying to imprison life, we often begin loving it more tenderly. Fragility itself becomes part of what makes things precious.

A quiet afternoon becomes holy because it will not come again in quite the same way. Autumn light across a familiar room suddenly feels sacred because we know it cannot be held forever. Even grief reveals something beautiful beneath the pain — that love once lived here deeply enough to wound us through its absence. Impermanence, though painful, softens the heart when we allow it to teach us gently.

And still, beneath all changing things, another longing continues calling from within. Not merely the longing for comfort or continuity, but for something untouched by decay itself. A goodness that does not disappear. A love that cannot be exhausted by loss. A peace deeper than circumstance, failure, aging, or death. The soul senses there must be something more enduring beneath the fragile surfaces of life.

The deeper spiritual traditions speak softly to this longing. They tell us there is a reality beneath appearances that does not pass away. A Presence deeper than every changing form. Not distant from the world, but quietly sustaining it from within — like the unseen depth beneath the waves of the sea. Human life rises and falls within vast movements we barely understand, yet beneath all becoming something remains unbroken, silent, and steady.

When a person begins resting inwardly in this deeper ground, they no longer demand permanence from temporary things. They can love without clinging. Serve without despair. Grieve without losing all hope. Endings still ache, and parting still wounds the heart, but another kind of steadiness slowly begins taking root beneath the movement of life. Like a harbor beneath storm-driven waters, something quiet remains.

Perhaps this is what the soul has always been seeking. Not endless possession. Not immortality for the ego. But Home. A resting in the Eternal Presence from which all beauty comes and toward which all longing quietly returns. The world remains precious, but it is no longer asked to carry what only the Eternal can hold.

### Breath Prayer

> Inhale: Anchor me beyond all change
>
> Exhale: Lead me home in love
