## 30 - Beyond the Returning

> *Everything that rises also passes away. But there is a Home beyond all endings.*

Everything visible carries within it the mark of impermanence.

Morning light fades into evening.
Flowers bloom and wither.
Children grow.
Bodies age.
Whole seasons of life arrive quietly and disappear before we fully understand they were passing.

Even joy changes form.

This is one of the deepest sorrows of being human:
to love what cannot remain exactly as it is.

And because the heart longs for permanence,
people often cling tightly to temporary things.

Relationships.
Achievements.
Identity.
Health.
Possessions.
Moments they wish could be frozen forever.

But life continues moving.

No amount of fear,
control,
or striving can stop the turning of the world.

The ancient wisdom traditions did not speak of impermanence to make the soul despair.

They spoke of it to loosen the grip of possessiveness.

For suffering grows heavier when we demand eternity from what was only ever meant to be a gift for a season.

Yet strangely, when we stop trying to imprison life,
we often begin loving it more tenderly.

A conversation becomes precious because it cannot last forever.
Autumn light across a quiet room becomes holy because it will pass.
Even grief reveals the depth of love that once existed.

Impermanence softens the heart when we allow it to teach us.

Still, beneath all changing things,
the soul senses another longing.

A longing not merely for comfort,
but for permanence itself.

For a goodness that does not decay.
A love that does not vanish.
A peace untouched by time,
failure,
or death.

The deeper spiritual traditions speak gently to this longing.

They tell us there is a reality beneath appearances that does not pass away.

A Presence deeper than every changing form.

Not distant from the world,
but holding it.

Like the unseen depth beneath the waves of the sea.

Human life rises and falls within vast movements we barely comprehend.

Generations come and go.
Civilizations appear and disappear.
Even the stars themselves are born and fade in time.

Yet beneath all becoming,
something remains.

Unbroken.
Silent.
Steady.

The soul was made for this deeper belonging.

Not as escape from life,
but as the ground from which life can be lived without fear.

When a person begins resting inwardly in what does not pass away,
they no longer demand permanence from temporary things.

They can love without clinging.
Serve without despair.
Grieve without losing all hope.

Because beneath every ending,
they trust there is still a deeper continuity held within Love itself.

This does not remove sorrow.

Parting still aches.
Death still wounds.
Change still humbles us.

But the heart slowly discovers another center beneath the movement of life.

A hidden steadiness.

Like a harbor beneath storm-driven waters.

And perhaps this is what the soul has always been seeking:
not immortality for the ego,
not endless possession,
but Home.

A resting in the Eternal Presence from which all beauty comes
and to which all longing quietly returns.

The world remains precious.
But it is no longer asked to carry what only the Eternal can hold.

### Breath Prayer

> Inhale: *Anchor me beyond all change*
>
> Exhale: *Lead me home in love*
