## 30 — Beyond the Returning

> *Everything that rises also passes away.  
> But there is a Home beyond all endings.*

## The Song

Krishna continued
to speak of remembrance.

The sages,
the seekers,
the disciplined ones
have long spoken of the Imperishable.

They restrain the senses.

They gather the mind inward.

They chant the sacred sound.

They long to cross beyond
the restless world of birth and death.

The doors of the senses are closed.

The mind is brought into the heart.

The life-breath is lifted upward.

The whole being becomes prayer.

And with the sacred syllable
rising from the depths,
the seeker remembers the Beloved
and leaves the body
moving toward the highest destination.

Yet Krishna gave a gentler promise.

The one who remembers Me always,
with an undivided heart,
easily comes to Me.

For such a soul
is never truly separated.

The path is not merely technique.

Not merely effort.

Not merely mastery
over hidden currents of breath and body.

It is love held steadily.

It is remembrance made constant.

It is the heart turned again and again
toward the One it trusts.

Those who reach Me,
Krishna said,
do not return
to this temporary house of sorrow.

They have reached the highest fulfillment.

For even the highest worlds
within creation
remain bound to return.

Even long ages end.

Even heavenly realms
pass through cycles
of arising and dissolving.

A day of Brahmā stretches beyond imagination.

A night of Brahmā stretches just as long.

Worlds appear with the dawn.

Worlds dissolve with the night.

Beings arise again and again,
drawn into form,
then gathered back into the unseen.

Creation breathes out.

Creation breathes in.

But beyond this vast rhythm,
beyond all appearing and disappearing,
there is another reality.

Unmanifest.

Eternal.

Unbroken.

When all worlds perish,
it does not perish.

This is the Supreme Abode.

The place from which there is no return.

The hidden Home
for which every longing
has been searching.

And that Supreme Person,
Krishna said,
within whom all beings dwell,
and by whom all things are pervaded,
is reached by undivided devotion.

### Along the Way

This passage stretches the imagination.

It places our brief lives
inside a universe of immense cycles —
birth,
death,
return,
dissolution,
renewal.

Everything we know changes.

Bodies change.

Relationships change.

Civilizations change.

Even worlds,
in the Gita’s vast vision,
rise and fall.

This can feel unsettling.

But it can also be clarifying.

Much of our suffering comes
from expecting temporary things
to provide permanent shelter.

We ask passing things
to become our final home.

We ask fragile bodies,
fragile plans,
fragile achievements,
and fragile securities
to hold the full weight of the soul.

But they cannot.

They were never meant to.

The Gita does not say this
to make us despise the world.

It says this
to free us from clinging.

When we understand
that all created things move through change,
we can love them more tenderly,
without demanding eternity from them.

We can receive life as gift
rather than possession.

We can hold beauty
without trying to imprison it.

We can grieve loss
without believing that loss
has the final word.

Krishna also makes the path deeply personal.

There are disciplined ways
of gathering the senses,
controlling the breath,
and preparing the mind
for the final passage.

But beneath all of this
is the simpler movement:

remember Me.

Love Me with an undivided heart.

Let your life become oriented
toward the One
who does not pass away.

This is not escapism.

It is the recovery of true center.

The heart that knows its eternal Home
can move through temporary places
with greater peace.

It can work,
love,
serve,
weep,
and rejoice
without being shattered
by every change.

Because beneath all endings,
there is a Presence that remains.

Beyond all returning,
there is a Love
that receives.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Anchor me beyond all change”

Breathing out:
“Lead me home in love”
