## 32 — The Presence Within All Things

> *The Divine is not far away.  
> The Holy breathes through all things seen and unseen.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke
of a mystery
too vast for the mind to hold.

I pervade the entire universe,
He said,
though unseen.

All beings rest within Me
as the wind rests within the sky.

Yet I am not confined by them.

Worlds arise within Me.

Worlds dissolve within Me.

Creation unfolds again and again
like waves upon an endless sea.

Forms appear.

Forms vanish.

Lives begin.

Lives end.

Still,
the Eternal remains untouched.

Unbound.

Free.

Material nature moves
under the gaze of the Divine.

The countless movements of life —
birth and decay,
growth and loss,
coming and going —
all unfold within this great mystery.

And yet humanity,
lost in distraction,
often fails to recognize
what is nearest.

Some mock the Divine
when it appears
in ordinary form.

They expect glory
to arrive clothed in power,
spectacle,
or certainty.

They do not recognize
the sacred hidden
inside humility,
inside tenderness,
inside human nearness.

But those whose hearts are open
begin to see differently.

They live with steady devotion.

They remember.

They bow inwardly
with gratitude.

They seek the Eternal
not only in temples,
rituals,
or sacred words,
but in the very fabric of existence itself.

And Krishna said:

I am the ritual
and the offering.

I am the sacred words
and the fire that receives them.

I am the father
and the mother.

The beginning
and the ending.

The shelter,
the witness,
the dear friend.

I am the heat of the sun
and the falling rain.

I am life
and death.

I am what is real
and what passes like shadow.

There is nowhere
the Divine is absent.

No breath untouched.

No moment empty
of sacred presence.

### Along the Way

This passage invites us
into a different way of seeing.

Many people imagine the spiritual life
as the search for extraordinary experiences.

Something dramatic.

Something distant.

Something removed from ordinary existence.

But the Gita turns our attention
toward a deeper realization:

the sacred is already here.

Not always obvious.

Not always emotionally overwhelming.

But quietly sustaining all things.

This changes how we move through the world.

If the Divine is present
within all life,
then every encounter matters.

Every person carries dignity.

Every moment becomes capable
of revealing something holy.

Even small acts —
listening carefully,
offering kindness,
speaking truth gently,
sharing a meal,
sitting beside someone in grief —
can become forms of devotion.

The passage also challenges
our tendency to divide life
into sacred and ordinary spaces.

We often believe
that only explicitly religious moments
carry spiritual meaning.

But the Gita widens the horizon.

The Divine is not merely found
inside rituals.

The Divine is the fire,
the offering,
the giver,
the receiver,
the breath beneath all of it.

This does not erase suffering
or confusion.

The passage openly acknowledges
illusion,
forgetfulness,
and blindness.

Human beings often fail
to recognize what is most essential.

We become captivated
by appearance,
status,
certainty,
or control.

But devotion slowly clears the eyes.

Not all at once.

Gradually.

The heart softens.

Awareness deepens.

And we begin to sense
that beneath the movement of life itself
there is a sustaining Presence
holding all things together.

A Presence closer
than we imagined.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“You are present here”

Breathing out:
“Open my eyes to see”
