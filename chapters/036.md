## 36 — The Light Hidden in All Beauty

> *The Eternal leaves traces everywhere.  
> Every glimpse of beauty can become remembrance.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke
of devotion
as living relationship.

To those who remain turned toward Me
with love,
I give the wisdom
that draws them nearer.

And out of compassion,
I enter their hearts
like a lamp lit in darkness.

Ignorance begins to loosen.

Fear begins to loosen.

The long night within the soul
begins to brighten.

Arjuna listened
with wonder.

You alone truly know Yourself,
he said.

Even the sages
cannot fully grasp Your mystery.

Tell me how I may remember You.

How should I see You
in this world?

And Krishna answered gently:

There is no end
to My expressions.

Still,
I will speak of a few.

I am the consciousness
within every living being.

The beginning,
the middle,
and the ending.

Among lights,
I am the radiant sun.

Among bodies of water,
I am the ocean.

Among mountains,
I am the Himalayas.

Among sounds,
I am the sacred syllable.

Among sacrifices,
I am the quiet repetition
of holy remembrance.

Among trees,
I am the great banyan.

Among all things
that awaken wonder,
strength,
beauty,
vastness,
or life —
there I am.

The Divine shimmered
through creation itself.

Not trapped within form,
yet reflected through it.

The Eternal speaking
through sunlight,
through mountains,
through wisdom,
through music,
through stillness,
through the living breath
inside every creature.

The world became
a thousand windows
opening toward the Infinite.

### Along the Way

This passage teaches
a sacred way of seeing.

The spiritual life is not only about
escaping the world.

It is also about learning
to perceive more deeply within it.

Many people move through life
half-awake.

Rushing.

Consuming.

Distracted.

The world becomes ordinary
because attention has grown dull.

But the Gita invites us
into holy attentiveness.

To notice.

To behold.

To recognize traces of the Eternal
woven throughout existence.

A mountain can awaken awe.

Music can soften the heart.

A great act of courage,
a moment of tenderness,
the vastness of the night sky,
the quiet intelligence of nature —
all can become reminders
of something greater
than the isolated self.

This does not mean
that every beautiful thing
is itself ultimate.

The Gita is careful about this.

The beauty points beyond itself.

Like sunlight through stained glass,
the glory is not trapped
inside the window.

Still,
the window matters.

Creation becomes a language
through which the soul
learns remembrance.

The passage also suggests
that meditation need not remain abstract.

Many people struggle
to hold attention on the invisible.

But the heart can be trained gently.

By pausing before beauty.

By receiving wonder.

By allowing ordinary moments
to become contemplative openings.

A quiet meal.

Rain against the window.

The warmth of another person's presence.

Birdsong at dawn.

The steady rhythm of breath.

Over time,
the world itself
begins to feel more alive.

Not because reality changed,
but because awareness deepened.

And perhaps this is part
of the healing the Gita offers:

to move through life
no longer sealed off
inside the prison of the ego,
but increasingly aware
that the sacred shines
through all things.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Open my eyes to wonder”

Breathing out:
“Let all beauty lead me home”
