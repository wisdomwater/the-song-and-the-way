## 53 — The Tree of Entanglement

> *The world can become a maze of attachment and illusion.  
> Freedom begins when the soul remembers what truly nourishes it.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke
of a vast and mysterious tree.

Its roots stretched upward.

Its branches spread downward
through the worlds of human life.

Its leaves whispered
with sacred teachings.

Its limbs spread endlessly
through desire,
memory,
ambition,
fear,
and longing.

Some branches reached upward
toward clarity and light.

Others descended
into confusion and attachment.

The tree was nourished
by the currents of nature—
goodness,
passion,
and ignorance.

And from its branches
hung the objects
of human fascination.

Pleasure.

Power.

Recognition.

Possession.

The endless cravings
that keep the heart
wandering among appearances.

Its roots spread deeply
into human life,
binding people
through action,
desire,
and consequence.

One choice leading to another.

One attachment feeding the next.

One fear becoming a prison.

Krishna said
this tree is difficult to fully comprehend.

Its beginning is hidden.

Its end cannot easily be seen.

Its tangled roots
run deep within the human soul.

And so the seeker
must take up
the strong blade
of detachment.

Not hatred of the world.

Not rejection of life.

But freedom
from compulsive clinging.

The courage
to loosen the grip
of ego and illusion.

To stop mistaking
temporary things
for eternal fulfillment.

Only then
can the soul begin
to seek the deeper source.

The root above.

The eternal reality
from which all life flows.

### Along the Way

This image of the upside-down tree
is one of the most powerful symbols
in the Gita.

Human life can easily become
a maze of entanglement.

We become rooted
in habits,
identities,
possessions,
social roles,
fears,
and desires.

Over time,
these attachments form
an entire inner world.

A complicated structure
of craving and reaction.

Much of modern life
encourages this entanglement.

We are trained constantly
to consume,
perform,
compare,
achieve,
and accumulate.

The branches keep multiplying.

More goals.

More distractions.

More noise.

Yet beneath it all,
many people quietly feel exhausted.

Disconnected.

Spiritually hungry.

Krishna does not condemn
the world itself.

The problem is not
that life exists.

The problem is forgetting
what life is rooted in.

When the soul loses connection
to the deeper source,
it begins searching everywhere else
for completion.

Status.

Pleasure.

Success.

Approval.

Control.

But none of these things
can finally satisfy
the deepest hunger of the heart.

This is why detachment
is so important.

Detachment does not mean
loving less.

It means clinging less.

Possessing less.

Allowing life
to be received as gift
rather than controlled as property.

A detached heart
can still enjoy beauty,
relationships,
work,
and joy.

But it no longer expects them
to carry the impossible burden
of becoming ultimate.

The “blade” Krishna describes
is the courage
to cut through illusion.

To question
what truly governs our lives.

To ask:
What am I rooted in?

What owns my attention?

What shapes my heart?

The spiritual journey
is not about escaping the world.

It is about learning
to live within it
without becoming completely lost inside it.

And somewhere beneath
all the tangled branches,
the soul begins to remember
its deeper origin.

Its deeper home.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Free me from illusion”

Breathing out:
“Root me in what is eternal”
