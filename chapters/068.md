## 68 — The Harvest of Our Actions

> *The quality of our actions shapes the quality of our consciousness.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke
of action.

Not all action
is the same.

Some actions
arise quietly
from clarity.

Done because
they are right.

Done without obsession
over reward.

Done without hatred.

Done without grasping.

These actions,
He said,
carry the fragrance
of goodness.

But other actions
are restless.

Driven by ambition.

Driven by the hunger
to be seen,
praised,
rewarded,
or remembered.

Performed with strain.

Performed to secure
some advantage.

These actions
belong to passion.

Then there are actions
born from confusion.

Careless actions.

Harmful actions.

Actions taken
without reflection,
without wisdom,
without concern
for consequences.

These arise
from darkness.

Krishna also spoke
of the one
who performs action.

The person grounded
in goodness
acts steadily.

Without arrogance.

Without despair.

Without becoming intoxicated
by success
or crushed
by failure.

But the person
caught in passion
is consumed
by outcomes.

Greedy for results.

Emotionally tossed
between excitement
and disappointment.

And the one trapped
in ignorance
moves through life
without discipline,
without clarity,
without inner direction.

Lazy.

Stubborn.

Resentful.

Lost in delay
and confusion.

Krishna says
the world we create
flows from the consciousness
we cultivate.

### Along the Way

This teaching feels
less like moral judgment
and more like diagnosis.

Krishna is not simply
labeling people
as “good” or “bad.”

He is describing
states of consciousness.

Ways of moving
through life.

And if we are honest,
we have likely lived
in all three.

There are days
when we act from clarity,
love,
and quiet integrity.

There are other days
when ego drives everything.

We chase validation.

We compare ourselves.

We work frantically
to prove our worth.

And sometimes
we simply shut down.

Numbness.

Avoidance.

Procrastination.

Escaping into distraction.

The invitation here
is not shame.

It is awareness.

To notice:
What energy
is shaping my actions?

Am I moving
from fear?

From greed?

From resentment?

Or from something deeper?

The modern world
often rewards
restless striving.

But the soul
cannot thrive
under constant inner agitation.

Eventually,
ambition without grounding
becomes exhaustion.

Consumption without meaning
becomes emptiness.

Action without wisdom
becomes destruction.

Krishna points toward
another possibility:

To act wholeheartedly,
yet without becoming
enslaved
to outcomes.

To do what is right
because it is right.

To serve without constantly
calculating reward.

To remain inwardly steady
whether life brings
success
or disappointment.

This is not passivity.

It is freedom.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Teach me to act with clarity”

Breathing out:
“Free me from restless striving”
