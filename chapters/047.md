## 47 — The Turning of the Heart

> *What we cling to shapes the soul.  
> What we continually love, we slowly become.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke
of the soul
moving through the world
of changing forces.

The human heart,
placed within material life,
becomes entangled
in its movements.

Drawn toward pleasure.

Pulled by fear.

Captured by craving,
status,
comfort,
identity,
and endless wanting.

The soul tastes
the moods of the world
and begins to forget
its deeper nature.

And because of this attachment,
it wanders.

Again and again
through cycles
of longing,
loss,
birth,
and sorrow.

But Krishna said
there is another Presence
within the body.

A sacred witness.

The silent companion
who sees all things.

The One who sustains life,
permits freedom,
and remains near
through every confusion.

Closer than thought.

Patient beyond measure.

Watching
as the soul searches
through countless desires
for what can only be found
in awakening.

And Krishna said:

Those who truly understand
the difference
between the restless movements
of the world
and the eternal Presence within
are no longer bound
in the same way.

Some discover this
through deep meditation.

In silence,
they descend beneath the noise
and glimpse
the light within the heart.

Others arrive
through reflection
and careful discernment.

They study the movements
of desire,
ego,
fear,
and illusion
until clarity begins to dawn.

Others awaken
through action offered selflessly.

By serving without clinging.

By giving
without constantly calculating
what will be returned.

And Krishna spoke tenderly
even of those
who do not fully understand.

Those who simply listen.

Those who hear
from awakened souls
and trust enough
to begin walking.

Even these,
He said,
cross beyond death.

For sincere longing
already turns the heart
toward freedom.

### Along the Way

This passage speaks
to one of the deepest truths
of spiritual life:

we become shaped
by what we continually attach ourselves to.

Attention is not neutral.

What we repeatedly feed
begins to form us.

If we live only for approval,
the soul becomes anxious and fragile.

If we live only for pleasure,
the heart becomes restless and unsatisfied.

If we live from fear,
the world slowly hardens around us.

But when attention
turns toward truth,
compassion,
stillness,
and love,
the inner life begins to change.

Krishna describes the human condition
with remarkable honesty.

The soul becomes entangled
in the moods of the world.

Anyone who has watched
their emotions rise and fall
already knows this experience.

One moment hopeful.

Another moment afraid.

One day peaceful.

The next consumed
by comparison or resentment.

Without awareness,
we are carried constantly
by these inner currents.

But the path begins
when we learn
to witness these movements
instead of blindly obeying them.

To pause.

To notice.

To ask:
What is shaping my heart?

This passage is also deeply compassionate.

Not everyone walks
the same spiritual road.

Some find truth in silence.

Others through contemplation.

Others through acts of love and service.

And some begin simply
through listening.

Through hearing wisdom
that awakens something ancient
inside them.

Krishna does not reserve freedom
only for intellectual mastery.

Even sincere openness matters.

Even imperfect steps matter.

Even longing matters.

The sacred often begins
not with certainty,
but with willingness.

A willingness
to turn inward.

To listen more deeply.

To loosen the grip
of the restless ego.

And to trust
that beneath all confusion,
something eternal
is quietly calling us home.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Turn my heart toward truth”

Breathing out:
“Lead me beyond fear”
