## 70 — The Work We Are Meant to Do

> *A healthy society honors the different gifts carried by different souls.*

## The Song

Krishna spoke
of the ways
human beings
naturally serve the world.

Some are drawn
toward wisdom.

Toward study,
reflection,
teaching,
prayer,
and truth.

These are the ones
who steady the mind
and seek understanding.

Others are drawn
toward courage.

Toward protection,
leadership,
responsibility,
and sacrifice.

These stand
between chaos
and the vulnerable.

Others cultivate
the fields,
care for animals,
build businesses,
create provision,
and sustain communities.

Others serve
through practical labor,
support,
craftsmanship,
and faithful work
that keeps daily life alive.

Krishna does not speak
of superiority.

He speaks
of function.

Different callings.

Different temperaments.

Different ways
the sacred moves
through human life.

A healthy society,
He says,
depends upon
honoring these differences
without turning them
into domination.

### Along the Way

This passage has often
been misunderstood
and misused.

Historically,
systems of hierarchy
have weaponized teachings
like this
to justify oppression,
especially through caste.

But Krishna repeatedly points
not to birth,
but to qualities,
actions,
and inner disposition.

The deeper invitation here
is not rigid social ranking.

It is discernment.

What kind of work
brings life
through me?

What responsibilities
fit the shape
of my soul?

Not everyone
is called
to lead publicly.

Not everyone
is called
to teach.

Not everyone
is called
to build businesses.

Not everyone
is called
to fight battles.

And this is not failure.

The modern world
often pressures people
to measure worth
through status,
wealth,
or visibility.

But a society collapses
when it loses reverence
for ordinary,
faithful contribution.

The person
who quietly cares for others.

The worker
who keeps things functioning.

The teacher
who nurtures wisdom.

The parent
who raises children
with tenderness.

The farmer.

The craftsman.

The nurse.

The protector.

The artist.

The servant-hearted soul.

All become threads
holding the human family together.

Krishna also warns
about what happens
when greed overtakes vocation.

When leadership
becomes domination.

When business
becomes exploitation.

When power
forgets compassion.

When profit
matters more
than life itself.

Then society
begins to devour itself.

The deeper question
is not:
“How important am I?”

But:

“How can my life
participate
in the healing
of the world?”

The sacred often moves
through very ordinary work.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Show me the work that is mine”

Breathing out:
“Let me serve with humility”
