## 26 — The Thread Through All Things

> *The world is not empty of the Sacred.  
> It is strung upon a hidden Love.*

## The Song

Krishna began to speak
of knowledge ripened into seeing.

Listen, He said.

Let your mind cling to Me.

Take shelter in Me.

Practice from this refuge,
and you will come to know Me —
not in fragments,
not as rumor,
not as distant thought,
but fully,
with the heart awake.

There is knowledge,
and there is realization.

There is truth heard from the outside,
and truth that becomes living fire within.

Krishna promised to speak of both.

And once this knowing settles in the soul,
nothing essential remains unknown.

Yet few seek this depth.

Among thousands,
one may begin longing for perfection.

Among those who long,
few truly come to know
the mystery as it is.

Then Krishna opened the world before Arjuna.

Earth.

Water.

Fire.

Air.

Space.

Mind.

Intelligence.

The false self
that says,
“I am separate.
I am the center.
I possess.”

All these,
He said,
belong to My material nature.

They form the visible and invisible fabric
of embodied life.

But there is another nature,
higher and more subtle.

The living consciousness.

The life within beings.

The presence that sustains the world.

From these two mysteries —
matter and life,
form and awareness —
all beings arise.

And Krishna said:

I am the beginning
and the dissolving
of the whole universe.

Nothing is higher than Me.

All things rest in Me
like jewels strung upon a thread.

The jewels may shine
with many colors.

They may appear separate.

But one hidden thread
holds them all.

Then He began naming Himself
within ordinary things.

I am the taste of water.

The light of the sun and moon.

The sacred sound
beneath all prayer.

The sound moving through space.

The fragrance of the earth.

The radiance of fire.

The life in every creature.

The strength of the strong
when strength is free from grasping.

The wisdom of the wise.

The brilliance of the radiant.

The seed of all beings.

Even the movements of goodness,
passion,
and darkness
arise from Me.

Yet I am not confined by them.

They live in Me.

But I am greater
than all that appears.

### Along the Way

This passage invites us
to see the world sacramentally.

Not as something to possess.

Not as something to escape.

But as something transparent
to a deeper Presence.

The taste of water.

The warmth of sunlight.

The smell of rain-soaked earth.

The clarity of wisdom.

The strength that protects
without dominating.

The breath moving through every living thing.

These are not distractions
from the spiritual life.

They can become doorways.

The Gita teaches that the Holy
is not absent from matter.

Nor is the Holy reduced to matter.

The world is held,
sustained,
and illumined
by a Reality deeper than what the senses can grasp.

This changes how we look.

A cup of water becomes more than water.

A flame becomes more than flame.

A human being becomes more than appearance,
status,
history,
or usefulness.

Every life is threaded
into a mystery larger than itself.

The image of jewels on a thread
is especially beautiful.

Most of the time,
we notice only the jewels.

This person.

This problem.

This desire.

This success.

This loss.

This moment.

But wisdom begins to perceive
the hidden thread.

The unity beneath difference.

The Presence quietly holding together
what appears scattered.

This does not flatten the world.

It deepens it.

Each thing remains itself,
yet becomes luminous.

The passage also names the false self.

That inner voice
that mistakes separation for truth.

That says,
“I am only this body.
I am what I own.
I am what I control.
I am above,
or beneath,
or apart.”

But the deeper life patiently loosens
that illusion.

We begin to remember
that we are not isolated fragments.

We are held within a living whole.

And when this becomes more than an idea,
gratitude begins to awaken.

Reverence returns.

The world becomes less like a warehouse
for our cravings
and more like a temple
filled with signs of hidden grace.

The spiritual life, then,
is not only looking upward.

It is learning to see rightly
what is already here.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Open my eyes to the thread”

Breathing out:
“Let me find You in all things”
