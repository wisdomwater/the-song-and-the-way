## 74 — The End of Fear

> *The purpose of true spiritual teaching is not dependence, but awakening.*

## The Song

Krishna said:

This wisdom
is sacred.

Not because it is secretive,
but because the heart
must be ready
to receive it.

Love cannot be forced.

Truth cannot be imposed.

The soul must hunger
for what is real.

And those who carry
this wisdom gently
to others —
not to control,
not to dominate,
not to elevate themselves —
but to awaken love —
become deeply dear
to the Divine.

Even those
who simply listen
with sincerity,
with openness,
with a willing heart,
begin crossing
the great ocean
of suffering.

Then Krishna turns
once more
to Arjuna.

Have you heard Me?

Has your confusion lifted?

Has your heart awakened?

And Arjuna answers:

Yes.

My illusion
has fallen away.

My memory
has returned.

I remember who I am now.

I remember
what matters.

I remember
why I am here.

My fear has loosened.

My doubts have quieted.

I am ready now
to walk forward.

And so,
the great dialogue ends.

Not with escape from life.

Not with withdrawal
from the world.

But with clarity.

With courage.

With a heart
reconnected
to the Divine.

### Along the Way

One of the most beautiful lines
in the entire Gita
is this:

“My delusion has been dispelled.”

Not:
“I have become perfect.”

Not:
“I understand everything.”

Not:
“I am spiritually superior.”

Simply:

“I can see clearly again.”

This is the movement
of awakening.

Much of suffering
comes from forgetfulness.

We forget
our deeper nature.

We forget
that we are more
than fear,
performance,
status,
or survival.

We forget
that beneath the noise
there is still
a sacred center
within us.

Arjuna’s “memory”
returning
is symbolic.

The soul
remembering itself.

And notice something important:

Krishna does not erase
Arjuna’s humanity.

Arjuna still has
responsibilities.

Still has grief.

Still has uncertainty.

But now
he no longer faces life
from confusion alone.

This matters deeply.

Authentic spirituality
does not remove us
from human life.

It transforms
how we inhabit it.

The Gita ends
not with domination,
but with relationship.

Not with blind obedience,
but with awakened participation.

Krishna teaches.

Arjuna listens.

Questions.

Struggles.

Resists.

Learns.

And finally chooses freely.

That freedom matters.

Love without freedom
is not love.

And perhaps
the final gift
of the Gita
is not merely doctrine,
but the possibility
that human beings
can awaken
from fear,
remember their deeper nature,
and walk through life
with courage,
clarity,
and love.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“My heart remembers”

Breathing out:
“I walk in peace”
