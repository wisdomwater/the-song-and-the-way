## 48 — Seeing the One Within All

> *The heart awakens when it no longer sees life as separate.  
> Compassion grows from recognizing the sacred everywhere.*

## The Song

Krishna said:

Everything that exists—
every creature,
every tree,
every moving life
and every silent thing—
arises through the meeting
of the field
and the knower within the field.

The world overflows
with forms.

Bodies rise and fade.

Mountains stand silently.

Birds cross the sky.

Rivers carve the earth.

Children are born.

Old hands tremble with age.

All life moves
through endless transformation.

Yet within every form
there shines
the same eternal Presence.

The wise learn
to see this.

Not merely
with the eyes,
but with the heart.

To see the Eternal
resting equally
within all beings.

Unbroken
even while bodies perish.

Unharmed
even while forms dissolve.

Krishna said:

The one who truly sees this
cannot continue living
through cruelty and domination.

For how can one destroy another
while recognizing
the sacred within them?

How can the heart
remain hardened
once it understands
that the same divine Presence
moves quietly
through all life?

The awakened soul
begins to loosen
its obsession
with superiority,
control,
and separation.

It no longer imagines itself
the center
of the universe.

It no longer believes
it owns life.

For it sees clearly
that the movements of the world
rise through forces
larger than the individual ego.

The body acts.

Nature moves.

Desire pulls.

Fear resists.

The world unfolds
through countless conditions.

And beneath it all,
the deeper Self remains—
watching,
unchanging,
silent as the sky.

Then Krishna spoke
of a great awakening.

The moment
when one no longer sees
life as fragmented and divided,
but perceives
all beings
resting within one sacred reality.

Like waves
belonging to one ocean.

Like sparks
emerging from one fire.

Like countless leaves
held by one living tree.

To see this
is to awaken
into freedom.

### Along the Way

The ego survives
through separation.

It constantly divides the world:

mine and yours,
worthy and unworthy,
friend and enemy,
higher and lower.

From these divisions
come pride,
violence,
greed,
and indifference.

But Krishna describes
a transformed vision.

A way of seeing
that recognizes
a deeper unity beneath life.

Not a denial
of individuality,
but the understanding
that all beings
participate in something sacred
that none of us created.

This vision changes
how we treat others.

When we truly see
another person
as carrying divine worth,
it becomes harder
to exploit,
humiliate,
or dismiss them.

Compassion becomes
less theoretical.

More natural.

And this widening compassion
does not stop with humanity alone.

The passage stretches the heart
toward all living beings.

Animals.

Creatures.

The vulnerable.

The forgotten.

Life itself
begins to appear
less like property
and more like gift.

Krishna also challenges
the illusion
that the ego
is fully in control.

Most of us move through life
saying:
“I did this.”
“I achieved this.”
“I failed.”
“I deserve.”

But much of what shapes us
comes from forces
we barely understand—
our upbringing,
our fears,
our wounds,
our environment,
our desires,
our conditioning.

This realization
does not remove responsibility.

It deepens humility.

It softens judgment.

It allows compassion
for ourselves
and for others.

Awakening begins
when we stop clinging
so tightly
to the isolated self.

When we begin to see
that beneath all appearances,
beneath all divisions,
something eternal
connects us.

And from that vision,
love grows wider.

Gentler.

Freer.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Help me see clearly”

Breathing out:
“In every living being”
