## 37 — Traces of the Infinite

> *Every true beauty carries a hidden radiance.  
> Every glimpse of greatness points beyond itself.*

## The Song

Krishna continued
revealing His presence
through the fabric of the world.

Wherever there is brilliance,
strength,
beauty,
wisdom,
or wonder,
there I am.

Among mountains,
the towering peaks.

Among rivers,
the sacred flowing waters.

Among warriors,
courage.

Among rulers,
right authority.

Among sounds,
the sacred vibration.

Among seasons,
the flowering spring.

Among creatures,
the lion.

Among birds,
the soaring eagle.

Among all things
that awaken awe,
something of the Divine
shines through.

Krishna spoke
as though the whole universe
were filled with echoes.

Nothing truly radiant
exists apart from the Eternal.

Every spark of goodness.

Every act of courage.

Every moment of truth.

Every expression of compassion.

Every form of beauty
is carried by a deeper Light.

And still,
Krishna reminded Arjuna
that these were only fragments.

A few reflections
of an immeasurable reality.

The Infinite cannot be exhausted
by descriptions.

The world overflows
with traces of the sacred,
yet the Source remains greater
than all appearances.

And finally Krishna said:

Why speak endlessly of particulars?

With only a fraction of Myself,
I sustain the entire universe.

The words fell silent
into mystery.

The soul stood
before something vast,
living,
and beyond containment.

### Along the Way

This passage teaches us
how to look at the world
without becoming trapped by it.

Human beings naturally attach themselves
to what is beautiful,
powerful,
successful,
or extraordinary.

We admire greatness.

We chase brilliance.

We long to possess
what awakens wonder in us.

But the Gita gently redirects
this impulse.

Do not stop
at the surface of beauty.

Let beauty become a doorway.

A glimpse.

A signpost.

The radiance we encounter in life
is not self-created.

It participates
in something deeper.

This changes the way we experience
both joy and desire.

Without this deeper awareness,
we cling desperately to forms.

We try to own beauty.

Control it.

Preserve it forever.

And suffering follows.

But when beauty becomes remembrance,
the heart softens instead of grasps.

Wonder becomes gratitude.

Awe becomes humility.

The soul learns to receive
without needing to possess.

The passage also invites us
into a more contemplative relationship
with ordinary life.

The sacred is not hidden
only in explicitly religious places.

It shines through creation itself.

Through music.

Through courage.

Through wisdom.

Through tenderness.

Through silence.

Through acts of mercy
that quietly restore dignity
to another human being.

The world becomes alive
with meaning
when we begin seeing this way.

Not because everything is perfect.

The Gita never denies suffering,
death,
or impermanence.

In fact,
Krishna openly says:
“I am death, the destroyer of all things.”

Even endings belong
within the mystery of existence.

This is difficult wisdom.

Yet strangely,
it can also become freeing.

Because the spiritual life
is no longer built upon denial.

It becomes the practice
of seeing the Eternal
moving through both beauty
and loss,
through beginnings
and endings,
through joy
and grief.

And beneath all these changing forms,
the Infinite remains.

### Breath Prayer

Breathing in:
“Let me see Your light”

Breathing out:
“In all that is beautiful”
