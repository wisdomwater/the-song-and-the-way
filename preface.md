## Preface

I have wanted to read the *Bhagavad Gita* for a long time.

As my own faith journey has unfolded from Christian evangelical roots, I have found myself becoming less interested in defending the particularities of one tradition against another, and more interested in listening for the deeper music that seems to move beneath them all.

Not because differences do not matter.

They do.

Every tradition carries its own language, imagery, history, wounds, beauty, and wisdom. But beneath those differences, I have come to believe there is a common thread — a quiet stream of truth flowing through humanity’s long search for God, meaning, awakening, and love.

If God is the Prime Mover, the original spark of life, the hidden Presence animating all things, then it seems reasonable to believe that no culture has been left entirely untouched by that Light. The same Spirit that breathes through creation also breathes through the human heart. The same mystery that stirs matter into life stirs the soul toward compassion, surrender, courage, wisdom, and love.

The mystics have always seemed to know this.

Across cultures and centuries, they speak different languages but often describe a strikingly similar encounter: the dissolving of fear, the widening of love, the humbling of the ego, the discovery of a Presence nearer than breath and greater than the mind can contain.

The word *Gita* means “song.”

And in the early Christian tradition, followers of Jesus were known as followers of “the Way.”

This book is my attempt to listen for the song within the way — to distill timeless principles from East and West into devotional reflections that can nourish the modern soul.

It is not meant to replace the sacred texts or traditions that inspired it. It is not a commentary for scholars or a system of doctrine. It is simply an invitation to slow down and listen for what is true beneath the noise.

Read these pages gently.

Do not rush.

Let your soul savor.

Let it rest.

And perhaps, somewhere along the way, you may hear the same quiet music that has been calling the human heart home since the beginning.
